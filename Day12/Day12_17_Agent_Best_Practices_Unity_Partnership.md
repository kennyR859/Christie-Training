## Problem-Solving Strategies

### Common Issues and Unity Solutions

**Issue #1: Appraisal Comes in Low**

**Unity Solutions:**
1. Pat negotiates with appraiser (points out missed comps)
2. Second appraisal option (Unity pays if higher)
3. Adjust down payment percentage (creative financing)
4. Portfolio loan if needed
5. Combination approach

**Agent's Role:**
- Provide strong comps to Pat before appraisal
- Stay calm and call Pat immediately
- Present all options to buyer
- Help negotiate with seller if needed

**Issue #2: Buyer's DTI Too High**

**Unity Solutions:**
1. Portfolio loan (up to 200% DTI possible)
2. Use rental income (75% of rent counts)
3. Pay off smaller debts to lower DTI
4. Co-borrower addition
5. Reduce loan amount slightly

**Agent's Role:**
- Identify DTI issues early (pre-approval stage)
- Work with Pat on creative solutions
- Help buyer understand options and trade-offs

**Issue #3: Self-Employed Buyer Shows Low Income**

**Unity Solutions:**
1. Portfolio loan (looks at full financial picture)
2. Bank statement loan programs
3. Asset-based lending
4. 1099 income evaluation
5. Business revenue consideration

**Agent's Role:**
- Send self-employed buyers to Pat early
- Set expectations (may need portfolio loan)
- Understand it's not a "problem" just different program

**Issue #4: Condo Not Fannie Mae Approved**

**Unity Solutions:**
1. Check FHA approval (different standards)
2. Portfolio loan option (Unity's independent review)
3. Work with association to get approved

**Agent's Role:**
- Check approval status BEFORE writing offer
- Alert Pat immediately if condo
- Request questionnaire Day 1 of contract

**Issue #5: Timeline Pressure**

**Unity Solutions:**
1. Expedite process where possible
2. Rush appraisal (fee may apply)
3. Parallel processing of tasks
4. Clear communication of realistic timeline

**Agent's Role:**
- Set realistic expectations from start
- Push buyer to respond quickly to requests
- Don't promise unrealistic timelines
- Communicate urgency to all parties
