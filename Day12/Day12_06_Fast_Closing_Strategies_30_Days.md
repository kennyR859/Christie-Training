## âš ï¸ When NOT to Promise Fast Closing

### Red Flags for Delays

**Customer Red Flags:**
- Slow to respond to emails/calls
- Disorganized (can't find documents)
- Complicated income (self-employed with irregular income)
- Weak credit or borderline approval
- Shopping multiple lenders simultaneously

**Property Red Flags:**
- Condo requiring questionnaire
- Known title issues
- Property needs repairs
- New construction or major renovations
- Multiple offers falling through previously

**Transaction Red Flags:**
- Attorney review likely to be contentious
- Seller has complicated situation
- Multiple parties involved (estate, trust, etc.)
- Liens or judgments to clear

### Pat's Honest Approach

**When Timeline Is Uncertain:**
> "I'm not going to issue a letter unless I know I can close it... I'm not going to ruin one deal just to make a lie and not close this deal"

**If Fast Closing Not Feasible:**
- Pat will tell you upfront: "This one will take 45-60 days"
- Better to set accurate expectations than over-promise
- Protects your reputation and relationship with listing agent
