## Buyer Pre-Approval Gimmicks

### The "Approved Without Property" Marketing Claim

**What It Really Is:**
Pat exposes the latest mortgage marketing gimmick: "Get approved without a property!" This is being advertised by some lenders as a revolutionary new product, but it's simply a glorified pre-approval letter with conditions.

**The Reality:**
- **No lender underwrites a loan without property information**
- Still subject to W2 verification, pay stub verification, tax return review
- Property must meet Fannie Mae guidelines
- Appraisal still required
- Full underwriting happens after property identified

**Why It's Misleading:**
```
Marketing Claim: "Approved without property"
Actual Reality: Pre-approval with standard conditions
Missing Elements: Property address, appraisal, title work, final underwriting
```

**Pat's Explanation:**
> "It's a glorified pre approval that they're just pointing on a commitment... no one is underwriting a loan without a property I can guarantee you that."

### What Real Underwriting Involves

**Income Verification Requirements:**
1. **W2 Employees:**
   - Last 2 years W2s
   - 1 month recent pay stubs
   - Employer verification (phone call or documentation)
   - 2 years personal tax returns

2. **Self-Employed:**
   - 2 years business tax returns
   - 2 years personal tax returns
   - Can add back depreciation to increase qualifying income
   - Pay stubs if taking W2 from own business

3. **Commission-Based:**
   - Base salary identified
   - Average commission last 2 years
   - Breakdown of income sources

**Property Verification:**
- Property must meet Fannie Mae guidelines (condition, location, condo approval)
- Appraisal required
- Title search required
- Full underwriting with property specifics

**The Effort Required:**
> "It takes hours to underwrite a loan to make sure the pace of it's right the W twos are right the income tax are right they got to follow DU and they're not going to put it down and you don't even know how much"

### Agent Takeaway

**When Clients Ask About These Programs:**
- Explain it's standard pre-approval with marketing spin
- Emphasize importance of full pre-approval process
- Direct them to Pat/Alexandria for honest assessment
- Real pre-approval requires detailed documentation upfront

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- Lenders promising "instant approval" without documentation
- Pre-approval letters without credit check
- No discussion of debt-to-income ratios
- No questions about employment type or income verification

## COVID Market Impact and Second Home Financing

### The Great NYC Exodus

**What Happened During COVID:**
The pandemic triggered unprecedented movement from NYC to NJ suburbs and second home markets. Customers couldn't use elevators safely, parks were closed, restaurants shut down - city life became untenable, especially for families with young children.

**Market Patterns:**
- NYC families moving to NJ suburbs 3-5 years earlier than planned
- Massive demand for homes with yards and pools
- Second home purchasing in beach towns and Poconos
- Properties with pools commanding premium prices

**The $30,000 Rental Story:**
> "Does anybody have a house with a pool in Alpine... I have a customer from New York who wants to rent it for the whole month of August and is willing to pay $30,000"

This actually happened during COVID - desperate NYC residents paying extreme premiums for private outdoor space.

### Second Home Financing Changes

**Pre-COVID (Normal Times):**
- Second home rates = Primary residence rates
- Standard down payment requirements
- Normal Fannie Mae processing

**Post-COVID (Current Reality):**
- **Second home rates are HIGHER than primary residence**
- Fannie Mae increased rates due to portfolio risk
- Concern: If market turns, people let go investment property first, then second home, then primary residence
- Portfolio became too heavy in second homes during pandemic

**Why Rates Increased:**
```
Problem: Too many second home mortgages in Fannie Mae portfolio
Solution: Can't say "no more second homes"
Result: Raised rates to make less attractive
Impact: Rates still elevated today
```

**Current Buyer Psychology:**
Even today, some buyers are purchasing second homes "just in case" another pandemic or crisis happens. They want to be prepared with a backup property featuring:
- Private yard
- Swimming pool
- Distance from city density
- Self-sufficient outdoor space

### Market Recovery and Inventory

**Current Inventory Issues (2025):**
- Low inventory due to COVID refinancing (many have 2.375% - 3.5% mortgages)
- Homeowners unwilling to give up ultra-low rates
- Market stuck waiting for rates to drop to "reasonable" levels

**Pat's Perspective:**

**When Inventory Will Improve:**

## Conventional Loans (Fannie Mae)

### What Are Conventional Loans?

**Definition:**
Conventional loans are Fannie Mae loans - the gold standard of residential mortgages. They're called "conventional" because they follow standardized Fannie Mae underwriting guidelines.

**Why They're the Best:**
- Lowest interest rates
- Lowest fees
- Lowest PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance)
- Most flexible terms
- PMI removable after 2 years if equity reaches 20%

> "These loans are the best loans kind of right for credit wise rate wise less fees less PMI and all that stuff"

### Qualifying Requirements

**Credit Score:**
- **Minimum:** 620 (Fannie Mae allows)
- **Recommended Minimum:** 680 (to avoid excessive PMI)
- **For 3% Down:** 720 credit score required

**Income Verification:**
- Must fit "the box" - structured income verification
- W2 employees: 2 years W2s, 1 month pay stubs
- Commission: Base + average 2-year commission
- Self-employed: 2 years personal and business tax returns
- **50% Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio maximum**

### DTI Calculation Example

```
Proposed Mortgage Payment:  $3,000/month
Current Monthly Debts:      $1,500/month
                           ________
Total Monthly Debt:         $4,500/month

Gross Monthly Income:       $9,000/month

DTI Ratio: $4,500 / $9,000 = 50%

Result: âœ… Qualifies (at maximum)
If over 50%: âŒ Cannot go conventional
```

### Down Payment Requirements

**Owner-Occupied Properties:**
| Property Type | Minimum Down Payment | Credit Score Requirement |
|---------------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| 1 Family | 3% | 720+ |
| 1 Family (standard) | 5% | 680+ |
| 2 Family | 5% | 680+ |
| 3-4 Family | 20% | 680+ |

**Investment Properties:**
| Property Type | Minimum Down Payment |
|---------------|---------------------|
| 1 Family | 20% |
| 2-4 Family | 25% |

**Important Note:**
While 3% down is possible, in multiple-offer situations, higher down payments win. The market reality since COVID has been that 20% down offers get accepted over 3-5% down offers.

### Property Requirements

**The property must also qualify:**
- Must meet Fannie Mae property condition guidelines
- If condo: Must be Fannie Mae approved OR questionnaire completed
- Cannot have significant deferred maintenance
- Must have clear title
- Must appraise at contract price

> "When you do a conventional loan say you want to buy a one family owner occupied you can do it with as little as 3% down If you buy a two family you can do a 5% down"

### PMI Removal Strategy

**How PMI Disappears (After 2 Years):**
1. Property appreciates to 20% equity position
2. Customer calls mortgage servicer
3. Servicer orders drive-by or BPO (Broker Price Opinion)
4. If value confirms 20% equity, PMI removed
5. **No refinance required if you have good rate**

**When to Refinance Instead:**
- If rates have dropped 1% or more since original loan
- If need to cash out equity for other purposes
- If want to change loan term (30-year to 15-year)
- Typical refi timeline: Every 18-24 months in normal markets

**COVID Exception:**
People with 2.375%-3.5% COVID rates will NEVER refinance to remove PMI - they'll just pay the small PMI to keep the ultra-low rate.

## Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

### What Is PMI?

**Definition:**
Private Mortgage Insurance protects the lender if borrower defaults on a loan with less than 20% down payment. It's required by Fannie Mae for all conventional loans under 20% down.

**Who Pays:**
The borrower pays PMI as part of their monthly mortgage payment until they reach 20% equity.

### Old PMI System (Pre-2015)

**Fixed Percentage Based Only on Down Payment:**
| Down Payment | PMI Percentage |
|--------------|---------------|
| 5% | 0.78% |
| 10% | 0.53% |
| 15% | 0.42% |
| 20%+ | 0% (no PMI) |

**Example Calculation (Old System):**
```
Loan Amount: $400,000
Down Payment: 5% ($20,000)
PMI Rate: 0.78%

Annual PMI: $400,000 Ã— 0.78% = $3,120
Monthly PMI: $3,120 Ã· 12 = $260/month
```

### New PMI System (Current 2025)

**Three-Factor Calculation:**
1. **Down Payment Percentage**
2. **Property Type** (single-family, condo, 2-4 family)
3. **Credit Score** â­ NEW FACTOR

> "PMI is based on down payment property type and credit score the higher the credit score the less PMI you get"

**Credit Score Impact:**
- **720+ Credit Score:** Lowest PMI rates
- **680-719 Credit Score:** Moderate PMI rates
- **620-679 Credit Score:** Highest PMI rates

**Why Credit Score Matters Now:**
PMI companies realized that high-credit borrowers default less frequently, so they reward good credit with lower insurance premiums.

### PMI Shopping Through DU

**DU (Direct Underwriting) System:**
Every conventional loan must run through Fannie Mae's DU system for approval. During this process, DU automatically shops 4-5 PMI companies and presents the best rate.

**Automatic Process:**
1. Loan submitted to DU
2. DU calculates risk based on: credit score, down payment, property type, DTI ratio
3. DU requests quotes from multiple PMI companies
4. DU presents 4-5 options
5. Lender clicks on best rate for customer
6. PMI automatically applied to loan

**Agent Benefit:**
You don't need to shop PMI - the system does it automatically and always gets the best available rate for that customer's profile.

### PMI Removal Options

**Option 1: Wait 2 Years + Appreciation**
- Property appreciates to 20% equity
- Call mortgage servicer after 2 years
- Request PMI removal
- Servicer orders drive-by appraisal or BPO
- PMI removed if 20% equity confirmed
- **No refinance required**

**Option 2: Refinance Anytime**
- Can refinance immediately if rates drop 1%+
- New appraisal required
- If new appraisal shows 20%+ equity, no PMI on new loan
- Must weigh closing costs vs PMI savings

**Option 3: Pay Down Principal**
- Make extra principal payments
- Once 20% equity reached, request PMI removal
- Usually requires new appraisal
- Good option if planning to stay long-term

### PMI Strategy for Agents

**Talking Points for Customers Concerned About PMI:**

4. **"PMI is tax-deductible for some borrowers"**

## FHA Loans

### What Are FHA Loans?

**FHA = Federal Housing Administration**

FHA loans are government-backed mortgages designed to help first-time homebuyers and those with lower credit scores or limited down payment funds access homeownership.

**Key Characteristics:**
- 3.5% minimum down payment
- 620 minimum credit score (technically 580, but most lenders require 620)
- Owner-occupied properties ONLY (no investment properties)
- MIP (Mortgage Insurance Premium) instead of PMI
- Higher DTI allowed (55% vs 50% conventional)

### When to Use FHA Loans

**Scenario 1: Limited Down Payment**
Customer only has 3.5% saved for down payment. Conventional requires minimum 3% BUT needs 720 credit score. If credit is 620-719, FHA is better option.

**Scenario 2: Lower Credit Score (620-680)**
FHA MIP is a flat percentage regardless of credit score. For customers with 620-680 credit, FHA MIP is actually CHEAPER than conventional PMI.

**Example:**
```
Customer Profile:
- Credit Score: 650
- Down Payment: 5%
- Loan Amount: $400,000

Conventional Loan PMI: ~$350/month (high due to low credit score)
FHA Loan MIP: ~$280/month (flat rate regardless of credit)

FHA Saves: $70/month Ã— 12 = $840/year
```

**Scenario 3: Need Higher Qualifying Amount**
Customer's debt-to-income ratio is 52% - too high for conventional (50% max) but acceptable for FHA (55% max).

**Example:**
```
Gross Monthly Income: $8,000
Maximum Monthly Debt:
- Conventional (50% DTI): $4,000
- FHA (55% DTI): $4,400

Extra qualifying capacity: $400/month
= ~$80,000 additional loan amount at 6% rate
```

**Scenario 4: Two-Family with Rental Income**
FHA allows 75% of rental income to be used for qualifying (conventional only allows 75% if renting for 2+ years).

**Example:**
```
Two-Family Purchase: $600,000
Customer's income: $6,000/month
Rental income potential: $2,000/month

FHA Calculation:
$6,000 (income) + $1,500 (75% of rent) = $7,500 qualifying income
Can afford higher mortgage payment

Conventional Calculation:
$6,000 (income) only
Lower qualifying amount unless can prove 2-year rental history
```

### FHA Limitations

**Owner-Occupied Only:**
- Must live in property as primary residence
- Cannot use for investment properties
- Must occupy within 60 days of closing
- Must remain primary residence for at least 1 year

**MIP Never Goes Away (for most loans):**
- Conventional PMI drops off after 2 years at 20% equity
- FHA MIP stays for LIFE OF LOAN if down payment under 10%
- Only way to remove: refinance to conventional once 20% equity reached

**Property Condition Requirements:**
- FHA appraisers must be specially licensed

## Appraisal Management

### The 2008 Crisis and Current Regulations

**What Happened in 2008:**
Prior to the financial crisis, mortgage lenders could hire appraisers directly. This created a conflict of interest:
- Lenders pressured appraisers for high values
- Appraisers inflated values to keep lender relationships
- Properties appraised for more than actual worth
- When market crashed, lenders lost billions on inflated mortgages

> "The problem was there was no checks and balances for appraisal so they would put whatever value they want... when the market crashed in 2008... all these appraisals were overhyped and the bank lost money"

**Post-2008 Regulation:**
All appraisers now belong to appraisal management companies (AMCs). Lenders order from AMCs, AMCs assign random appraisers. This prevents:
- Direct lender-appraiser relationships
- Pressure to inflate values
- Cherry-picking friendly appraisers

### Unity Bank's Appraisal Advantage

**Local Appraisers:**
Unity uses AMCs that employ local NJ and PA appraisers who know the market.

**Competitor Disadvantage:**
Big national banks (Chase, Wells Fargo, Bank of America) use national AMCs that send out-of-state appraisers unfamiliar with local market nuances.

**Example:**

- Doesn't understand Ridgewood school district premium
- Doesn't know neighborhood micro-markets
- Under-appraises property

**Paul the Appraiser:**

## Seller Concessions Strategy

### What Are Seller Concessions?

**Definition:**
Seller concessions are when the seller agrees to pay a portion of the buyer's closing costs. This is done by RAISING the sales price and having the seller credit money back to the buyer on paper.

**Important:** The credit is a PAPER CREDIT on the closing disclosure, not a check handed to the buyer.

### How Seller Concessions Work

**Example:**
```
Buyer's Situation:
- Has 5% down payment saved: $25,000
- Does NOT have closing costs: $15,000 needed
- Total cash needed: $40,000
- Only has: $25,000

Solution - Seller Concession:
Step 1: Raise sales price by $15,000
- Original agreed price: $500,000
- New contract price: $515,000

Step 2: Add language to contract
- "Sales Price: $515,000"
- "Additional Comments: $15,000 seller concession towards buyer's closing costs"

Step 3: Buyer brings down payment only
- 5% of $515,000 = $25,750
- Closing costs ($15,000) credited on closing disclosure
- Buyer brings: ~$25,750 vs original $40,000

Step 4: Two things must happen:
âœ… House must APPRAISE for $515,000
âœ… Buyer must QUALIFY income-wise for $515,000 loan
```

**Key Point:**
The seller still gets their original $500,000. The extra $15,000 goes to pay closing costs, so it's a wash for the seller. But buyer can proceed without the extra $15,000 cash.

### Maximum Seller Concession Limits (Conventional Loans)

**By Down Payment Percentage:**

| Down Payment | Maximum Concession |
|--------------|-------------------|
| 5% down | 3% of purchase price |
| 10-15% down | 3% of purchase price |
| 10-25% down | Up to 6% of purchase price |
| 25%+ down | Up to 9% of purchase price |

**Examples:**

Closing costs rarely reach 9% of purchase price unless it's a very low-priced home or unusual situation.

### The 4506 Form - Tax Transcript Verification

**What It Is:**
IRS Form 4506-T authorizes lender to pull customer's actual tax returns directly from IRS.

**Old System:**
- Customer signed 4506 form
- But lenders rarely executed it
- Customers could provide fake tax returns
- Fraud was rampant

**Current System (Post-2008):**
- Customer signs 4506 form
- **Lender MUST execute it** (verify with IRS)
- IRS sends official tax transcript
- Lender compares customer-provided returns to IRS transcript
- Any discrepancy = loan denied + fraud reported

**Why This Matters to Agents:**
Tell customers upfront: "Whatever you show the lender will be verified with the IRS. Don't exaggerate or alter tax returns - it's federal fraud."

### Red Flags Pat Watches For
