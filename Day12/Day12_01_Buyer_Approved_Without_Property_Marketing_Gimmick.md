## üìã Overview

One of the latest mortgage marketing gimmicks being advertised is "Get approved without a property!" or "Buyer approved with no property required!" This sounds revolutionary and appealing to buyers eager to shop without commitment. However, it's essential for real estate agents to understand what this really means and educate customers accordingly.

**Key Message:** There is NO such thing as a true mortgage approval without property information. What's being marketed is simply a glorified pre-approval letter with standard conditions.

## ‚ùå The Reality: What It Actually Is

### Still Just a Pre-Approval

**Pat's Explanation:**
> "It's a glorified pre approval that they're just pointing on a commitment... no one is underwriting a loan without a property I can guarantee you that."

**What "Approved Without Property" Really Means:**
```
Marketing Claim: "Approved without property"
Actual Reality: Pre-approval with standard conditions

Missing Elements:
- Property address
- Appraisal
- Property inspection
- Title search
- Final underwriting
- Actual loan approval
```

**Still Subject To:**
- W2 verification
- Pay stub verification
- Tax return review
- Credit re-check at closing
- Property meeting Fannie Mae guidelines
- Appraisal at contract price
- Full underwriting after property identified

### Why No Lender Can Truly Approve Without Property

**Underwriting Requirements:**

**1. Property Must Meet Guidelines:**
- Fannie Mae has specific property condition standards
- Condo must be Fannie Mae approved or have questionnaire completed
- Property cannot have deferred maintenance issues
- Structure must be sound and habitable

**2. Appraisal Required:**
- Loan amount based on lower of: purchase price OR appraised value
- Cannot determine loan amount without knowing property value
- Appraisal required by federal lending regulations

**3. Title Must Be Clear:**
- Cannot lend on property with title defects
- Liens must be identified and resolved
- Title insurance required before closing

**4. Debt-to-Income Includes Property:**
- Property taxes affect DTI calculation
- HOA fees (if condo) affect DTI calculation
- Homeowners insurance cost affects DTI calculation
- Final loan amount may differ from pre-approval

## üîç What REAL Underwriting Involves

### Income Verification Process

**For W2 Employees:**
1. **Last 2 years W2s** - Verify employment history and income stability
2. **1 month recent pay stubs** - Confirm current employment and pay
3. **Employer verification** - Lender calls employer or requests written verification
4. **2 years personal tax returns** - Cross-check income reported

**For Self-Employed:**
1. **2 years business tax returns** - Verify business income
2. **2 years personal tax returns** - Verify personal income
3. **Can add back depreciation** - Increases qualifying income
4. **Pay stubs if taking W2 from own business** - Additional verification

**For Commission-Based:**
1. **Base salary identified** - Stable income component
2. **Average commission last 2 years** - Variable income calculated
3. **Breakdown of income sources** - Understand compensation structure

### The Time and Effort Required

**Pat's Perspective:**
> "It takes hours to underwrite a loan to make sure the pace of it's right the W twos are right the income tax are right they got to follow DU and they're not going to put it down and you don't even know how much"

**What Underwriters Verify:**
- Income tax returns match IRS records (4506-T form)
- W2s match employer records
- Pay stubs are legitimate (not fabricated)
- Bank statements show sufficient funds
- Credit report is accurate
- Debt-to-income ratio is within guidelines
- Employment is stable and continuing
- No undisclosed debts or obligations

**Timeline:**
A thorough underwriting process takes:
- 3-5 days for initial review
- 7-10 days to collect all conditions
- 2-3 days for final underwriting approval
- **Total: 2-3 weeks minimum**

No lender is doing this without knowing the property details, because many factors depend on the specific property.

## üö© Red Flags to Watch For

### When Customer Has This Type of "Approval"

**Warning Signs:**
1. **No Property Address Listed**
   - Letter says "approved for up to $X amount"
   - No specific property mentioned
   - Generic pre-approval language

2. **"Subject To" Language**
   - "Subject to acceptable property"
   - "Subject to appraisal"
   - "Subject to final underwriting"
   - "Subject to verification of income and employment"

3. **No Credit Check Mentioned**
   - Doesn't state credit score used
   - Doesn't show credit was pulled
   - Generic "subject to credit approval" language

4. **No Lender Contact Information**
   - Just company name, no specific loan officer
   - 1-800 number instead of direct line
   - No local office or presence

5. **Suspiciously Quick Approval**
   - "Approved in 5 minutes!"
   - No documentation requested yet
   - Customer hasn't provided tax returns, pay stubs, etc.

### Questions to Ask Customer

**If they show you this type of letter:**

1. **"Have they pulled your credit yet?"**
   - Real pre-approval requires hard credit inquiry

2. **"What documents have you provided?"**
   - Should include: pay stubs, W2s, tax returns, bank statements

3. **"Have you spoken to a specific loan officer?"**
   - Should have direct contact, not call center

4. **"What's the exact loan amount approved?"**
   - Should be specific number, not "up to $X"

5. **"Did they ask about the type of property?"**
   - Should discuss single-family vs condo vs multi-family

## üìä Industry Context

### Why This Marketing Exists

**Business Motivation:**
- Mortgage industry is highly competitive
- Companies need to differentiate themselves
- Attention-grabbing headlines generate leads
- Many consumers don't understand lending process

**The Problem:**
- Misleading marketing creates false expectations
- Customers think they're "approved" when they're not
- Deals fall apart when reality hits
- Agents waste time on unqualified buyers

### Historical Context

**Pre-2008 Crisis:**
- "Stated income" loans existed
- No verification of income or assets
- Could get approved without documentation
- Led to massive fraud and market crash

**Post-2008 Regulations:**
- Every loan must be fully documented
- Income must be verified with IRS (4506-T form)
- Assets must be sourced and seasoned
- Credit must be re-pulled before closing
- Property must meet strict guidelines

**Current Reality:**
Lenders trying to sound like pre-2008 ease without actually violating current regulations. Result: Misleading marketing that overpromises.
