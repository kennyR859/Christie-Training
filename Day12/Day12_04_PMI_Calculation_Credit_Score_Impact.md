**Major Change:** PMI calculation changed significantly around 2015. It's no longer just about down payment percentage - credit score now plays a huge role.

## ðŸ’° What Is PMI?

### Purpose and Function

**Definition:**
Private Mortgage Insurance protects the LENDER (not the borrower) if the borrower defaults on a loan with less than 20% down payment.

**Why It Exists:**
- Fannie Mae requires it for all loans under 20% down
- Reduces lender's risk on low down payment loans
- Allows buyers to purchase with less than 20% down
- Makes homeownership accessible to more people

**Who Pays:**
The borrower pays PMI as part of their monthly mortgage payment until 20% equity is reached.

**How Much:**
Typically $150-$400/month depending on:
- Loan amount
- Down payment percentage
- Property type
- **Credit score** â­ (biggest factor now)

## ðŸ†• New PMI System (Current 2025)

### Three-Factor Calculation

**Pat's Explanation:**
> "Now PMI is based on down payment property type and credit score the higher the credit score the less PMI you get"

**The Three Factors:**

**1. Down Payment Percentage**
- 3% down = Highest PMI
- 5% down = High PMI
- 10% down = Moderate PMI
- 15% down = Lower PMI
- 20%+ = No PMI

**2. Property Type**
- Single-family = Standard PMI
- Condo = Slightly higher PMI
- 2-4 family = Higher PMI
- Investment property = Highest PMI

**3. Credit Score â­ NEW FACTOR**
- 760+ = Lowest PMI rates
- 720-759 = Low PMI rates
- 680-719 = Moderate PMI rates
- 620-679 = High PMI rates

### Why Credit Score Matters Now

**PMI Company Logic:**
PMI companies analyzed their loss data and discovered:
- High credit score borrowers default FAR less frequently
- Even with low down payment, 760+ credit score = very low default rate
- Low credit score borrowers default more frequently
- Even with 10-15% down, 640 credit score = higher default rate

**Result:**
PMI companies reward good credit with significantly lower rates.

## ðŸ”„ DU (Direct Underwriting) PMI Shopping

### Automatic PMI Shopping System

**How It Works:**

**1. Loan Submitted to DU:**
Every conventional loan must run through Fannie Mae's DU (Direct Underwriting) system for approval.

**2. DU Calculates Risk:**
Based on:
- Credit score
- Down payment
- Property type
- DTI ratio
- Reserves
- Other risk factors

**3. DU Shops PMI Companies:**
DU automatically requests quotes from 4-5 PMI companies:
- MGIC
- Radian
- Essent
- National MI
- Others

**4. DU Presents Options:**
Shows lender the 4-5 PMI quotes ranked by cost.

**5. Lender Selects Best Rate:**
Clicks on lowest rate option for customer.

**6. PMI Applied to Loan:**
Automatically added to monthly payment calculation.

### Agent Benefit

**You Don't Need to Shop PMI:**
- System does it automatically
- Always gets competitive rate
- No need to compare PMI companies manually
- Customer gets best available rate for their profile

**Pat's Role:**
- Pat submits to DU
- DU shops and presents options
- Pat selects lowest cost for customer
- All automated and fast

### Credit Score Boost Tactics

**1. Pay Down Credit Card Balances**

**How It Works:**
Credit utilization (balance Ã· limit) significantly impacts credit score.

**Example:**
```
Credit Card Limit: $10,000
Current Balance: $8,000
Utilization: 80% (BAD)

Action: Pay down to $4,000
New Utilization: 40% (BETTER)

New PMI (715 score): $220/month
Savings: $60/month = $720/year

Cost to Improve: Pay down $4,000 (money you have anyway)
```

**Pat's Approach:**
- Looks at credit report during pre-approval
- Identifies high-balance credit cards
- Calculates how much to pay down for score boost
- If customer pays down before final underwriting, score improves
- Lower PMI rate locked in

**2. Don't Close Credit Cards**

**Common Mistake:**
Customer thinks: "I'll close these credit cards to look better"

**Reality:**
Closing credit cards LOWERS your score because:
- Reduces total available credit
- Increases utilization percentage on remaining cards
- Shortens credit history

**Example:**
```
Before Closing Cards:
Total Credit Limit: $30,000
Total Balance: $5,000
Utilization: 17% (GOOD)
Score: 720

After Closing $15,000 in Cards:
Total Credit Limit: $15,000
Total Balance: $5,000
Utilization: 33% (WORSE)
Score: 695-705 (dropped)
```

**Pat's Instruction:**
> "Do not close your credit cards OK because now when you clothing up now it's been when you close your credit cards even though they're paid to zero your credit score goes lower"

**3. Get New Statement with Lower Balance**

**Process:**
```
Timeline:
Week 1: Pay down credit cards
Week 2: Wait for statement to generate with new lower balance
Week 3: Print statement showing new balance
Week 4: Pat sends to credit company
Week 5: Credit re-scored with new balance
Week 6: New score used for rate/PMI determination
```

**When This Matters:**
If customer is right on edge between credit score tiers (e.g., 678 score, need 680):
- Pay down cards immediately
- Get new statement
- Re-score before rate lock
- Can save thousands in PMI

- Property appreciates enough to reach 20% equity
- Want to remove PMI early (before 2 years)

**Aggressive Paydown Strategy:**

**Who This Works For:**
