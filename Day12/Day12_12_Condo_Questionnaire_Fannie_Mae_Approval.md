## Overview

Condo financing is more complex than single-family home financing. Fannie Mae requires condo associations to meet specific criteria before approving loans. Understanding the condo questionnaire process helps agents avoid deals that cannot close and properly set buyer expectations.

---

## Why Condos Are Different

### Single-Family Home vs Condo Financing

**Single-Family Home:**
- Property stands alone
- Buyer responsible for entire property
- Lender evaluates individual property only
- Straightforward qualification

**Condo:**
- Part of larger association
- Shared ownership of common areas
- Association financial health matters
- Association must be Fannie Mae approved
- **Additional layer of qualification**

**Key Difference:**
> Even if the buyer qualifies perfectly and the individual unit appraises well, the loan CANNOT close if the condo association is not Fannie Mae approved.

---

## What Is the Condo Questionnaire?

### Purpose

**Fannie Mae's Concern:**
- Is the association financially stable?
- Are reserves adequate for maintenance?
- Is the association properly managed?
- Are there legal issues or special assessments?
- Is owner-occupancy ratio healthy?

**The Questionnaire:**
- Detailed form about association finances and operations
- Must be completed by association management
- Reviewed by Fannie Mae underwriters
- Determines if association meets lending standards

**Who Completes It:**
- Condo association management company
- Or HOA president if self-managed
- **NOT** the seller, buyer, or agent

## The Approval Process

### Timeline

**Ideal Scenario:**
```
Week 1: Request questionnaire from association
Week 2: Association completes and returns
Week 3: Lender submits to Fannie Mae
Week 4-6: Fannie Mae reviews and approves
```

**Reality:**
```
Week 1-2: Association slow to respond
Week 3-4: Association submits incomplete questionnaire
Week 5: Lender requests additional info
Week 6-8: Resubmission and Fannie Mae review
Week 9-10: MAYBE approval
```

**Pat's Warning:**
> "Condo questionnaires can delay closing by 4-6 weeks MINIMUM. If association is uncooperative or has issues, it can kill the deal entirely."

## Common Problems

### Problem 1: Association Won't Cooperate

**Scenario:**
- Small condo association (10-20 units)
- Self-managed by owners
- No professional management company
- Don't understand questionnaire importance

**Result:**
- Delayed responses
- Incomplete information
- Frustration for all parties
- Potential deal failure

**Solution:**
- Start questionnaire request IMMEDIATELY at contract
- Buyer's agent should contact association directly
- Explain urgency and importance
- Consider offering to pay rush fee ($200-500)

### Problem 2: Association Not Approved

**Scenario:**
- Association has never been through approval process
- Unknown financial status
- Possible disqualifying factors

**Result:**
- 6-8 week review process
- Uncertain outcome
- Buyer and seller stuck waiting
- May ultimately be denied

**Solution:**
- Check if condo is on **Fannie Mae Approved Condo List** before writing offer
- If not on list, disclose lengthy process to buyer
- Consider contingency for condo approval

### Problem 3: Association Recently Denied

**Scenario:**
- Condo was previously reviewed
- Failed to meet Fannie Mae requirements
- Financial issues, lawsuits, or ratio problems

**Result:**
- **CANNOT GET CONVENTIONAL FINANCING**
- FHA might work (different standards)
- Cash buyers only
- Values significantly impacted

**Agent Responsibility:**
- Research condo approval status early
- Don't waste buyer's time on un-financeable property
- Disclose financing limitations

### Problem 4: High Rental Ratio

**Scenario:**
- Building is 60-70% investment rentals
- Only 30-40% owner-occupied
- Violates Fannie Mae 50% requirement

**Result:**
- Fannie Mae will NOT approve
- Conventional financing unavailable
- FHA may work with different standards
- Portfolio loan possible at Unity

**Warning Signs:**
- High turnover building
- Many units listed for rent
- Investor-heavy area
- Vacation/seasonal rentals

## When Condo Is NOT Approved

### Alternative Financing Options

**Option 1: FHA Condo Approval**
- Different standards than Fannie Mae
- May approve what Fannie Mae denies
- BUT: FHA has own condo questionnaire
- Still lengthy process

**Option 2: Unity Portfolio Loan**
- Unity can keep loan in portfolio
- Not bound by Fannie Mae condo requirements
- More flexible standards
- Typically requires:
  - 20-25% down payment
  - Good buyer credit (680+)
  - Condo financially stable (even if not Fannie approved)

**Option 3: Cash Purchase**
- No financing = no condo approval needed
- Only option if all financing denied
- Significantly reduces buyer pool
- Impacts property values

## How Long Does It Take?

### Best Case Scenario

**Condo Already Fannie Mae Approved:**
- Minimal delay (1-2 weeks)
- Just verify approval still valid
- Proceed like normal transaction

**Total Time:** 30-45 days to close (normal timeline)

### Typical Scenario

**Condo Not on Approved List:**
- Questionnaire request: Week 1
- Association completion: Weeks 2-4
- Lender submission: Week 5
- Fannie Mae review: Weeks 6-10
- Approval (hopefully): Week 10-12

**Total Time:** 60-90 days to close

### Worst Case Scenario

**Association Uncooperative or Denied:**
- Extended delays (12+ weeks)
- Multiple resubmissions
- Potential denial
- Switch to alternative financing or terminate

**Total Time:** 90+ days OR deal falls apart

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Property Red Flags

- ‚ùå Building is primarily rentals (>50%)
- ‚ùå Significant commercial space (>25%)
- ‚ùå Many units for sale (financial distress?)
- ‚ùå Deferred maintenance visible
- ‚ùå Special assessment recently passed or planned

### Association Red Flags

- ‚ùå Self-managed (no professional company)
- ‚ùå Very small association (<10 units)
- ‚ùå High HOA fees relative to amenities
- ‚ùå Low reserves or special assessments
- ‚ùå Pending litigation
- ‚ùå Insurance lapsed or claims history

### Process Red Flags

- ‚ùå Association slow to respond
- ‚ùå Listing agent doesn't know approval status
- ‚ùå Previous deals fell through (financing issues?)
- ‚ùå Seller anxious to close fast (knows about problems?)

## Communicating with Buyers

### Setting Expectations

**At First Showing:**
> "I want you to know that condo financing is more complex than single-family homes. The condo association must be approved by Fannie Mae, which can add 4-8 weeks to the process. We'll check the approval status before making an offer."

**Before Offer:**
> "I checked, and this condo is [approved/not approved] by Fannie Mae. If not approved, we're looking at a 60-90 day timeline instead of the typical 30-45 days. Are you comfortable with that timeline?"

**If Problems Arise:**
> "The condo association is having difficulty completing the questionnaire. This may delay our closing by several weeks. Pat is working with them, but I want to keep you informed. We have options if this becomes a major delay."

## Best Practices for Agents

### Do Your Homework Early

1. **Check approval status** before showing condo
2. **Research association** management and reputation
3. **Ask listing agent** about past financing experiences
4. **Review condo docs** early (budget, minutes, rules)

### Communicate Proactively

1. **Start questionnaire** request on Day 1 of contract
2. **Follow up consistently** with association
3. **Update all parties** on status weekly
4. **Alert to delays** as soon as discovered
5. **Coordinate** between buyer, lender, association

### Protect Your Commission

1. **Don't write offers** on un-financeable condos
2. **Verify approval** before investing time
3. **Set realistic timelines** in contract
4. **Include contingencies** for questionnaire approval
5. **Have exit strategy** if denial occurs
