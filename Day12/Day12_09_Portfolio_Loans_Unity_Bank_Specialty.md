## ðŸ“Š Portfolio Loan Requirements

### Credit Score
- **Minimum:** 680
- **Preferred:** 700+
- Lower scores case-by-case with strong compensating factors

### Down Payment
- **Owner-Occupied:** 20% minimum
- **Investment:** 25% minimum
- Sometimes flexible with exceptional profile

### Reserves
- **Required:** 6-12 months mortgage payments
- In bank, 401K, or investments
- More reserves = more flexibility

### Business/Employment History
- 2+ years in business preferred
- Tax returns required (to see full picture)
- Bank statements to verify cash flow
- Must make sense for their industry

## ðŸ†š Portfolio vs Hard Money

### Hard Money Lenders
- **Down Payment:** 30-35%
- **Interest Rate:** 12%
- **Points:** 2-3 points (2-3% of loan)
- **Income Verification:** None needed
- **Term:** Short-term (6-12 months)
- **Use Case:** Terrible credit, no income proof, lots of cash

### Unity Portfolio
- **Down Payment:** 20-25%
- **Interest Rate:** Market + 0.5-1%
- **Points:** 0-1 point
- **Income Verification:** Required (but flexible)
- **Term:** 30-year fixed
- **Use Case:** Good credit, doesn't fit conventional box

**When to Use Each:**
- **Portfolio:** Customer has good credit, decent income, just doesn't fit Fannie Mae
- **Hard Money:** Terrible credit, no verifiable income, but lots of cash and short-term plan

## ðŸŽ¯ How to Know When to Suggest Portfolio

### Red Flags for Conventional

**Income Issues:**
- Self-employed showing low taxable income
- Multiple income streams hard to document
- Foreign income
- Recent job change to different industry
- Commission-based with declining trend

**DTI Issues:**
- Over 50% debt-to-income
- High student loan payments
- Multiple properties
- Child support/alimony

**Credit Issues:**
- Recent bankruptcy (discharged but still on report)
- Recent foreclosure (over 3 years ago)
- Settled debts showing on report
- Credit score 650-680 range

**Property Issues:**
- Condo not Fannie Mae approved
- Unique property type
- Mixed-use property
