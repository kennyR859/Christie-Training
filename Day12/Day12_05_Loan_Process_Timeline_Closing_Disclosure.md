### Phase 8: Closing Disclosure (CD)

**Timeline: Minimum 3 Days Before Closing**

**ðŸš¨ FEDERAL LAW: CD Must Be Issued 3 Days Before Closing**

**What Is the CD:**
Legal document showing:
- Final loan amount
- Interest rate
- Monthly payment (principal + interest)
- Escrows (taxes and insurance held monthly)
- All closing costs itemized
- Cash to close (total amount buyer brings)
- Seller credits and concessions

**3-Day Rule:**
```
Example Timeline:
Monday 9am: CD issued to customer
Monday 9am: Customer must sign CD
Thursday: Earliest possible closing day

Cannot close Tuesday or Wednesday - must wait 3 full business days
```

**Why 3 Days Required:**
- Federal consumer protection law
- Gives buyer time to review all costs
- Prevents last-minute rate/fee changes
- Prevents predatory lending practices

**If Anything Changes:**
Must issue new CD and restart 3-day clock. Changes include:
- Interest rate change
- Loan amount change
- Fee increases
- APR change over 0.125%

### Key Sections of CD

**Page 1: Loan Terms**
- Loan amount
- Interest rate
- Monthly principal & interest
- Does rate/payment change? (No for fixed)

- Balloon payment? (No for 30-year fixed)

**Page 1: Projected Payments**
- Principal & Interest: Fixed amount
- Mortgage Insurance (PMI): Shows amount and when it can be removed

- Estimated Total Monthly Payment

**Page 2: Closing Costs**
- Loan Costs (origination, points, appraisal, credit report, title services)
- Other Costs (taxes, insurance, HOA fees if applicable)
- Total Closing Costs
- Cash to Close calculation

**Page 3: Detailed Cost Breakdown**
- Every single fee itemized
- Shows what borrower pays vs what seller pays
- Agent commissions shown

**Page 5: Loan Calculations**
- Total of Payments over loan life
- Finance Charge
- Amount Financed
- Annual Percentage Rate (APR)

## âš ï¸ Common Delays and How to Prevent

### Delay #1: Missing Commission Statement

**Problem:** Agent forgets to request commission statement

**Impact:** CD cannot be issued, closing delayed

**Prevention:**
- Set calendar reminder 1 week before closing
- Request from Admin immediately
- Follow up to ensure sent to title company
- Verify title company received it 3 days before closing

### Delay #2: Customer Opens New Credit

**Problem:** Customer buys furniture or car during process

**Impact:** DTI increases, may no longer qualify

**Prevention:**
- Pat warns customers during pre-approval
- Agent reinforces: "NO new credit until after closing"
- Tell customers upfront this can kill the deal

**Pat's Instructions:**
> "Do not buy furniture Do not buy a car do not put extra money on your credit cards do not close your credit cards"

### Delay #3: Large Deposits

**Problem:** Customer deposits $20,000 cash into bank account

**Impact:** Must source and document (cannot use cash)

**Prevention:**
- Warn customers: NO large deposits during process
- If unavoidable, document BEFORE depositing
- Gift money: Keep in parent's account until instructed

### Delay #4: Job Change

**Problem:** Customer changes jobs during process

**Impact:** Must re-verify employment, may delay or kill deal

**Prevention:**
- Tell customers: Stay at current job until after closing
- If unavoidable, same industry/same salary = usually okay
- Different industry or lower salary = major problem

### Delay #5: Rate Lock Expires

**Problem:** Process takes longer than rate lock period

**Impact:** Rate may increase if market rates went up

**Prevention:**
- Lock rate for appropriate period (45-60 days typical)
- Unity doesn't charge to extend lock
- But new rate may be higher if market moved
