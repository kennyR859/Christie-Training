## 3. NAR Settlement Rules: Buyer Agency Agreements (August 2024)

### The Core Question (From Lecture)

**Agent's Dilemma:**
> "They never signed the buyer's agency agreement because I didn't feel comfortable the second they walked into the open house like 'hey can you sign this'... nowadays, other than showing them more properties, like they never signed the buyer's agency agreement."

### Instructor's Critical Clarification

**The Rule:**
> "The law is that they don't have to sign a buyer's agreement at an open house. But at an open house we make it really clear: **you may not sell the house to anybody, you only show the house, you're working for the seller.** You do not have to do a buyer agency agreement done at an open house."

**Why This Rule Exists:**
> "You should not have buyer agency agreements done at an open house. **You're not supposed to do any transaction business at the open house.** The open house is just an open house to expose it to the public and see if there's interest."

### What You CAN Do at Open House

**Allowed Activities:**
1. **Show the property** to unrepresented buyers
2. **Answer questions** about the property
3. **Provide property information** (features, neighborhood, schools)
4. **Collect contact information** (QR codes, sign-in sheets)
5. **Schedule follow-up** conversations

**Working for the Seller:**
> "At an open house, you're working for the seller, and that's it. You do not work with buyers at that open house. You do not get contracts, anything."

### What You CANNOT Do at Open House

**Prohibited Activities:**
1. ❌ Sign buyer agency agreements
2. ❌ Write contracts or offers
3. ❌ Represent buyers in transaction
4. ❌ Negotiate on buyer's behalf
5. ❌ Provide buyer-specific advice

## 4. When Buyer Agency Agreements ARE Required

### The Timing Rule (NAR Settlement - August 17, 2024)

**2025 Requirement:**
> "A written agreement between an agent and a buyer would be necessary **prior to that agent taking the buyer on any home tours**, unless state law requires that the agreement be signed earlier in the process."

### Specific Scenarios

**1. Showing Other Properties (REQUIRES AGREEMENT)**

**From Lecture:**
> "If they want to see another house, then **you must do a buyer agency agreement.**"

**When Agreement Required:**
- Scheduling private showings
- Touring properties not at open house
- Providing buyer representation services
- Searching for properties on buyer's behalf
- Negotiating offers for buyer

**2. Open House Follow-Up (REQUIRES AGREEMENT)**

If buyer from open house wants to:
- See other properties with you
- View same property again privately
- Write an offer with your representation
- Have you act as their buyer's agent

**Then you MUST:**
- Schedule meeting to sign buyer agency agreement
- Explain agency relationship
- Disclose compensation structure
- Sign agreement BEFORE showing additional properties

**3. Open House Itself (NO AGREEMENT NEEDED)**

**Exception:**
> "Unrepresented buyers are not required to enter into a written buyer agreement to attend an open house."

**Why:**
- Agent is working for seller, not buyer
- Just providing property access
- No buyer representation occurring
- Open to general public

## 5. Listing Agent's Special Obligations

### The Recent Clarification (From Lecture)

**Instructor's Statement:**
> "There was something else that came through, I don't want to muddy the water too much, but when you are the **primary listing agent** and you're going to lead, your **obligation is to share that house whether you have a signed agency agreement or not.**"

**Clarification:**
> "You're the listing agent, I have 1 First Street for sale, I get this call 'I want to go see the house.' **An obligation to show that house for the seller** because you're the primary listing agent. You do not have to sign the buyer agency agreement."

### When This Applies

**Scenario:**
- You're the listing agent
- Unrepresented buyer calls
- Wants to see YOUR listing
- You can show them without buyer agency agreement

**Why:**
- You're working for seller (your client)
- Showing seller's property to potential buyers
- Not "working with" the buyer
- Performing listing duties

**From Research (2025):**
> "A broker who is 'providing an unrepresented buyer access to a house they have listed' is an example of someone who is **not 'working with' a buyer**."

### What "Working For Seller" Means

**Ministerial Acts (No Agreement Needed):**
- Showing the property
- Providing property information
- Arranging access
- Answering basic questions
- Presenting offers to seller

**Buyer Representation (Agreement REQUIRED):**
- Searching for properties for buyer
- Arranging tours of multiple properties
- Providing buyer-specific advice
- Negotiating on buyer's behalf
- Performing comparative market analysis for buyer

## 6. After the Open House: Next Steps

### If Buyer Wants to Write Offer on Open House Property

**Two Options (From Lecture):**

**Option 1: After Open House, Same Day**
> "You can have contracts in your folder, you can sit down and write them out **right after the open house**. You can do it in the house except it has to be, if it's one to 4, it would have to be closed, and the door has to be closed to the public."

**Requirements:**
- Open house officially ended
- Door closed to public
- Property is 1-4 family (owner-occupied or investment)
- Can write contract for open house property without buyer agency agreement

**Option 2: Schedule Follow-Up Meeting**
- Meet at office or neutral location
- Review property and offer strategy
- Write contract with proper documentation
- Close open house property without buyer agency agreement (allowed)

**From Research (2025):**
> "The governor said you're working for the seller, and if they do want to buy that house or they do want to make an offer for that house, you can do it **after the open house without the buyer agency agreement.**"

### If Buyer Wants to See Other Properties

**MUST Sign Buyer Agency Agreement First:**
1. Schedule meeting (office, coffee shop, their home)
2. Explain buyer representation
3. Disclose compensation structure
4. Review and sign buyer agency agreement
5. THEN schedule showings

**From Lecture:**
> "If they do want to see another house then **you must do a buyer agency agreement.**"

## 7. Compliance & Investigations

### The Warning (From Lecture)

**New Enforcement:**
> "What happens when they show up, when the **investigators show up at the open house?** There were **33 new investigators hired for Northern New Jersey** to check us out, make sure we're doing the right stuff."

### How to Stay Compliant

**The Folder System:**
> "I will give you a folder, I don't know if you've got the folder with you or not, but I'll give you a folder where **you can zip it up and hide everything in there including your laptop and your phone and it will be hidden.**"

**What to Hide During Open House:**
- Buyer agency agreement forms
- Contract forms (until after open house closes)
- Laptop (with transaction documents)
- Phone (if contains sensitive info)
- Any buyer representation materials

**What You Can Have Visible:**
- Property information sheets
- Neighborhood information
- Sign-in sheet / QR code
- Your business cards
- Seller disclosure forms
- Listing materials

**Why This Matters:**
> "It's easy to do the right stuff, you just **don't have any papers there**, you just show the house, see if there's any interest, tell them **'I can help you right after the open house.'**"

### If Investigator Arrives

**What They're Looking For:**
- Are you signing buyer agency agreements at open house?
- Are you representing buyers during open house?
- Are you writing contracts during active open house?
- Are you violating open house rules?

**How to Handle:**
- Remain professional and cooperative
- Show property information only
- Explain you're showing for seller
- Note: transaction documents are secured
- Offer to discuss any questions after open house ends

## 8. Limited Property Representation Agreement (New Option)

### 2025 Solution for Open Houses

**From Research:**
> "There is a new type of agreement designed specifically for open houses that is limited to just the open house property and lasts for 30 days."

**Limited Property Representation and Broker Compensation Agreement:**

**Key Features:**
- **Non-exclusive:** Buyer can work with other agents
- **Property-specific:** Applies only to open house property
- **Time-limited:** No longer than 30 days
- **Simplified:** Easier than full buyer agency agreement

**When to Use:**
- Buyer very interested in open house property specifically
- Wants your representation for this property only
- Doesn't want full buyer agency relationship yet
- Wants to make offer with your help

**When Not Needed:**
- Just touring open house
- No representation requested
- Not writing offer
- Seller representation only

### Follow-Up Call Script (For Interested Buyers)

**Opening:**
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. I'm following up from the open house at [Address] yesterday. Do you have a moment to chat?"

"I'd love to help you with that! To show you other properties, I would need to represent you as your buyer's agent. This is for the property you saw at the open house, so we can proceed without a separate buyer agency agreement for this transaction. When are you available?"

## 11. Common Scenarios & How to Handle

### Scenario 1: "Can you show me other homes in this area?"

**Your Response:**
"I'd be happy to help you! To show you other properties and represent you as a buyer, I need to meet with you first to sign a buyer agency agreement. This outlines our working relationship and compensation structure. Could we schedule 20-30 minutes this week?"

**Why:**
- Showing other properties = buyer representation
- Requires buyer agency agreement FIRST
- Cannot show other properties from open house without agreement

### Scenario 2: "Can we write an offer right now?" (During Open House)

**Your Response:**
"I'm glad you're interested! The open house needs to stay open to the public right now. I can stay after [end time] and we can close the door and write up the offer then. Or we can schedule a time later today or tomorrow. Which works better for you?"

**Why:**
- Cannot do transaction business during active open house
- Can write offer after open house closes
- For THIS property (open house property), no buyer agency agreement needed

### Scenario 3: "I want to see this house again, just me and my spouse."

**Your Response:**
"Of course! Since this is my listing, I can schedule a private showing for you anytime. Let me check my calendar - what days work best for you this week?"

**Why:**
- You're listing agent for this property
- Can show YOUR listing without buyer agency agreement
- Still representing seller, not buyer

### Scenario 4: "Are you my agent or the seller's agent?"

**Your Response:**
"Great question! At this open house, I'm the listing agent representing the seller. I'm here to show you the property and answer questions about it. If you'd like me to represent you as a buyer for this or other properties, we would need to meet and sign a buyer agency agreement. Either way, I'm happy to help!"

**Why:**
- Clarity prevents confusion
- Sets proper expectations
- Explains dual pathway (listing agent vs. buyer agent)
- Complies with disclosure requirements

### Scenario 5: "Do I need to sign anything to look at this house?"

**Your Response:**
"No, not at all! Open houses are open to the public. I just ask that you sign in with this QR code [or sign-in sheet] so I can follow up with any additional information you might want. Feel free to look around and let me know if you have any questions!"

**Why:**
- No agreement needed for open house attendance
- Sign-in is for follow-up, not legal requirement
- Keeps process simple and welcoming
