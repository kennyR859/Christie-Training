## âš–ï¸ The New Jersey Law (August 2024)

### What Changed

**OLD (Before August 2024):**
- Buyer could see properties without signing anything
- Verbal agreements were common
- Agency disclosure at first substantive contact

**NEW (After August 2024):**
- **Any buyer viewing a property MUST sign agreement FIRST**
- Exception: Open houses (no agreement required)
- Governor Murphy signed into law
- Applies to ALL New Jersey agents

---

### Why This Law Exists

**Background:**

1. **NAR Settlement (2024):**
   - National Association of Realtors lawsuit settlement
   - Clarifies buyer representation and compensation
   - Increases transparency

2. **New Jersey State Addition:**
   - Governor added state-specific requirements
   - Stricter than NAR baseline
   - Penalties for non-compliance

**Purpose:**
- Clarify agency relationships
- Protect consumers
- Prevent dual agency confusion
- Ensure buyers know who represents them

## ðŸ“‹ Required Forms (In Order)

### Form 1: CIS (Consumer Information Statement)

**What It Is:**
- Consumer Information Statement
- Explains different types of agency in NJ
- MUST be presented BEFORE buyer agency agreement

**Topics Covered:**
- Sub-agency (rare now)
- Buyer agency (most common)
- Dual agency (problematic)
- **Designated agency** (recommended solution)

**When to Present:**
- At the property, before entering
- Can email/text ahead of time (better)

**How Long:**
- They should read it (2-3 minutes)
- You explain key points (1 minute)
- They acknowledge receipt

---

### Form 2: Buyer Agency Agreement

**What It Is:**
- Contract between buyer and agent
- Specifies scope of representation
- Defines compensation
- Sets time period

**Must Include:**
- Property address (for limited agreements)
- Duration (one day, one week, or ongoing)
- Exclusive vs. non-exclusive
- Compensation amount/structure
- Termination clause

## ðŸ“ Presenting the Agreement

### The Script (Non-Threatening)

**Step 1: Acknowledge the Law**

```
"New Jersey passed a law in August requiring all buyers to have
a written agreement before seeing properties. It's to protect you
and clarify who's working for you."
```

**Step 2: Emphasize Limited Scope**

```
"I've made this agreement as simple as possible - it's only for
today and only for this one house.

After today, it expires. No long-term commitment."
```

**Step 3: Highlight Their Freedom**

```
"If after today's showing you want to work with someone else,
you're completely free to do so. This doesn't lock you into
anything beyond today."
```

**Step 4: Explain the Purpose**

```
"The main purpose is so you know that I'm working FOR YOU,
not for the seller. I'm legally required to represent YOUR
best interests in this transaction."
```

**Step 5: Ask for Signature**

```
"Any questions about it before we sign?"

[Address questions]

"Great! Just need your signature here and we can go in."
```

## âš ï¸ Common Objections & Responses

### Objection 1: "I don't want to sign anything"

**Wrong Response:**
```
"Well, I can't show you the house without it."
[Confrontational, kills rapport]
```

**Right Response:**
```
"I totally understand - nobody likes signing things the first
time they meet someone.

Here's the situation: New Jersey law requires it before I can
show you any property. It's not my rule, it's the state's.

The good news is this agreement expires tonight at midnight.
So it's really just for this one showing.

If you like the house and want to work together going forward,
we can talk about a longer agreement later. But for now,
this is just to let us walk through the door legally."
```

**If they still refuse:**
```
"I understand. Unfortunately, I can't show you the property
without this agreement - I could lose my license.

If you'd like, I can email you all the information about the
house, and you can drive by on your own. When you're ready
to see inside, we can schedule another time.

Does that work?"
```

**Most will sign at this point.**

---

### Objection 2: "I'm already working with another agent"

**Response:**
```
"That's perfectly fine! Many buyers work with multiple agents,
especially when looking in different areas.

This agreement is non-exclusive, which means you can work with
other agents too. It's only for this specific property today.

Your other agent can still show you other properties.

Does that make sense?"
```

**If they push back:**
```
"Since you already reached out about this specific house,
let's at least see it today. This agreement covers just
this showing.

After today, you can decide who you want to work with going forward."
```

---

### Objection 3: "What if I want to buy this house?"

**Response:**
```
"Great question! If you love the house today and decide to
make an offer, we'd extend the agreement to cover the purchase.

But we can discuss that AFTER you see it. No point in worrying
about that unless you actually like it, right?

Let's go in, see if it's even worth considering, and then
we can talk about next steps."
```

## ðŸ“‹ Different Agreement Types

### Option 1: One-Day, One-Property (UPCity Leads)

**Best For:**
- First-time meeting
- UPCity leads
- Skeptical buyers
- Testing compatibility

**Duration:** Today only
**Scope:** This property only
**Type:** Non-exclusive

**Form Language:**
```
This agreement is effective from [today's date] through [today's date]
and applies only to the property located at [address].

This is a non-exclusive agreement.
```

---

### Option 2: One-Week, Multiple Properties

**Best For:**
- After first successful showing
- Buyer wants to see more
- Building relationship
- They're motivated but cautious

**Duration:** 7 days
**Scope:** Any properties you show them
**Type:** Non-exclusive

**Form Language:**
```
This agreement is effective from [start date] through [end date, +7 days]
and applies to all properties shown by Agent during this period.

This is a non-exclusive agreement.
```

---

### Option 3: Full Exclusive Buyer Agreement

**Best For:**
- After 2-3 showings
- Buyer is committed
- You've built rapport
- Ready to actively search together

**Duration:** 3-6 months
**Scope:** All properties in specified area
**Type:** Exclusive

**Form Language:**
```
This agreement is effective from [start date] through [end date, +90 days]
and applies to all properties in [geographic area / price range].

This is an EXCLUSIVE agreement. Buyer agrees to work exclusively
with Agent during this period.
```

**When to transition:**

After 2-3 showings:
```
"You seem serious about finding a house, and I'd love to help you.

To really serve you well, I'd like to formalize our working relationship
with a full buyer agreement.

This ensures I'm dedicating time to finding you the perfect house,
and you're committed to working with me.

It's typically for 3-6 months. If at any point you're unhappy with
my service, we can cancel it. Does that work?"
```
