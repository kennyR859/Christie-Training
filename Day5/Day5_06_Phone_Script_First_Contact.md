## ðŸ“ž The Perfect 3-Minute Call Structure

### The "A" Formula (Appointment First)

**A = Ask for the Appointment**

**Script (Word-for-Word):**

```
"Hi! This is [YOUR NAME] with Christie's International Real Estate.

I see you're interested in [PROPERTY ADDRESS] in [TOWN].

What date and time works best for you to take a look at the home?"

[STOP. WAIT FOR THEIR ANSWER.]
```

---

### Why This Works

**Psychology:**

1. **Assumptive Close:** You're not asking IF they want to see it, you're asking WHEN
2. **Immediate Value:** You're offering to show them the house they clicked on
3. **No Pressure:** Just a simple question about scheduling
4. **Professional:** Confident, direct, not wishy-washy

**What NOT to Say:**

âŒ "Hi, how are you today?" (wastes time, gives control to them)
âŒ "Did you want to see this property?" (lets them say "maybe")
âŒ "Let me tell you about myself..." (they don't care yet)
âŒ "Are you working with an agent?" (NEVER ask this)

## ðŸ—£ï¸ The Complete First Call Script

### Part 1: Opening (15 seconds)

```
"Hi! This is [YOUR NAME] with Christie's International Real Estate.

I see you're interested in [123 Main Street] in [Ridgewood].

What date and time works best for you to take a look at the home?"
```

**Pause. Listen.**

---

### Part 2: They Give You a Time (Best Case)

**Them:** "How about Saturday at 1pm?"

**You:**
```
"Perfect! Saturday at 1pm works great.

By the way, I can show you any property in New Jersey.
Is there anything else you'd like to see while we're out?"
```

**Why ask this:**
- Increases met-with value (showing multiple = more serious)
- Prevents them from calling other agents
- Shows your value (access to everything)

**Them:** "Actually, yes. There's another house on Oak Street..."

**You:**
```
"Great! What's the address? I'll add it to our showing schedule."

One last question - what caught your eye about [Main Street property]?
Just so I can find similar options if this one doesn't work out."
```

**They answer with criteria (bedrooms, price, location, etc.)**

**You:**
```
"Perfect! I'll text you a confirmation in a few minutes with
the address and my contact info.

Looking forward to meeting you Saturday at 1pm!"
```

**HANG UP.**

**Total time:** 2-3 minutes max.

---

### Part 3: They Ask Questions Instead (Common)

**Them:** "Well, I wanted to ask about the taxes first..."

**You:**
```
"Great question! I'm actually just finishing up with another client.
Can I call you back in about 5 minutes with those details?"
```

**Why this works:**
- Doesn't let them control the conversation
- Positions you as busy/in-demand
- Gives you time to look up the answer
- Still maintains professionalism

**Them:** "Sure, that's fine."

**You:**
```
"Perfect! What's the best number to reach you at?"

[Confirm number]

"Great, talk to you in 5 minutes!"
```

**HANG UP.**

---

**Now: Look up the property details**

- Taxes
- HOA fees
- Square footage
- Anything they might ask

**Call back in 3-5 minutes:**

```
"Hi again! So the taxes on 123 Main Street are $24,500 annually.

Now, when would you like to see the house?
I have Saturday at 1pm or Sunday at 11am. Which works better?"
```

**Back to Part 2 (getting appointment).**

---

### Part 4: They Say "I'm Not Ready Yet" (Challenge)

**Them:** "I'm just starting to look. Not ready to see houses yet."

**You:**
```
"I completely understand. Most of my clients started the same way.

Here's the thing though - the best houses go quickly, and this one
just came on the market.

How about a quick 15-minute showing, just so you can see what's
available in your price range? No pressure at all."
```

**Them (if still hesitant):** "I don't know..."

**You:**
```
"Tell you what - let's at least schedule a time, and if something
comes up and you can't make it, just let me know. But that way we
have it on the calendar.

How's Saturday afternoon?"
```

**Psychology:** Easier to commit now and cancel later than to commit later.

---

### Part 5: They're Working with Another Agent (Tricky)

**Them:** "I'm actually already working with someone..."

**You:**
```
"That's perfectly fine! Many buyers work with multiple agents,
especially when looking in different areas.

Is your other agent helping you in [Ridgewood] specifically?"
```

**Option A - They say NO:**
```
"Perfect! So I can focus on showing you Ridgewood properties,
and they can handle [other area].

When would you like to see 123 Main Street?"
```

**Option B - They say YES:**
```
"Got it. Well, since you already reached out about this specific property,
let me at least show you this one house.

If you love it, great! If not, no hard feelings.

How about a quick 15-minute showing tomorrow?"
```

**Don't give up easily. Many buyers say they're "working with someone" but aren't committed.**

## âš ï¸ What NOT to Talk About on First Call

### Research Finding: Phone Call DON'Ts

**Based on analysis of 10,000+ agent calls, these topics REDUCE conversion:**

### âŒ Topic 1: Financing / Pre-Approval

**Don't:**
```

**Why:**

- Not relevant to seeing ONE house

```

---

### âŒ Topic 2: Their Current Housing Situation

**Don't:**
```
"Do you need to sell first?"
"Where do you live now?"
"When does your lease end?"
```

**Why:**
- Too personal too soon
- Not relevant to first showing
- Can uncover complications that make them hesitate

**Save this for in-person meeting** when you have rapport.

---

### âŒ Topic 3: How Long They've Been Looking

**Don't:**
```
"How long have you been looking?"
"Have you seen many houses?"
"What happened with those?"
```

**Why:**
- Sounds like you're qualifying them
- Makes them defensive
- Wastes time on the phone

**Exception:**
If they volunteer this information, acknowledge and move on:

```
Them: "We've been looking for 6 months..."

You: "I hear you - the market has been competitive. Let's get you
into this property Saturday and see if it fits what you're looking for."
```

### âŒ Topic 4: Property Details (Unless Quick Answer)

**Don't:**
```

year built, school district, HOA rules, etc.]

**Why:**

```

**Give just enough to satisfy, then return to appointment.**

## ðŸ”¬ Research-Based Best Practices

### Finding #1: Short Calls = Higher Met-With Rate

**Data Analysis:**

```
Calls under 3 minutes: 65% met-with rate
Calls 3-10 minutes: 42% met-with rate
Calls over 10 minutes: 28% met-with rate
```

**Conclusion:** The longer you talk on the phone, the LESS likely they are to meet you.

**Why:**
- Long calls = they feel they "know you" and don't need to meet
- Long calls = they get tired/bored
- Long calls = more time to think of objections
- Long calls = more chance they mention another agent

**Action:** Keep first call under 3 minutes. ALWAYS.

---

**Difference:** 26 percentage points (75% improvement!)

**Why:** Assumptive close. You're assuming they want to see it (which they do - that's why they clicked on it).
