### What is Remine?

Remine is a **data intelligence platform** that allows agents to:
- Identify homeowners with specific characteristics (equity, loan type, ownership duration)
- Build targeted farming territories based on selling indicators
- Export contact data for CRM integration
- Track neighborhood trends and market opportunities

> **Think of Remine as:** A search engine for potential sellers, where you filter by motivation triggers instead of just location.

---

### ðŸŽ¯ Advanced Filtering Strategies

#### Strategy 1: ARM Loan Targeting (High Probability Sellers)

**The Opportunity:**
Homeowners with Adjustable Rate Mortgages (ARMs) locked in at low rates (under 4.5%) will see their payments **potentially double** when rates reset.

**How to Find Them in Remine:**

1. **Property Value Filter:** $1,000,000+
2. **Ownership Time:** 20+ years
3. **Loan Type:** ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage)
4. **Interest Rate:** Under 4.5%

**Example Result:** 29 households

**Why This Works:**
```
ARM at 2% interest â†’ Resets to 7% interest
$5,000/month payment â†’ $10,000/month payment
Result: Many homeowners cannot afford doubled payments = MUST SELL
```

**Your Script:**
> "Hi, I'm calling the neighborhood. I have a buyer interested in purchasing in the area. Do you know anyone who's looking to sell?"

They respond: "Actually, I've been thinking about it..."

**Why not say "Are you selling?"** Because you want THEM to volunteer the information, not feel pressured.

---

#### Strategy 2: Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC) Targeting

**The Signal:**
A second mortgage (HELOC) often indicates:
- Financial need (pulled cash out)
- Delaying a sale (needed money but didn't want to sell yet)
- Potential motivation to sell now

**Remine Filters:**
- Property Value: $1,000,000+
- HELOC: Yes
- Equity: Maximum $200,000 (meaning large loan)

**The Math:**
```
Home Value: $1,000,000
Equity: $200,000
â†’ Loan: $800,000 (high debt)
â†’ Rate changes affect them significantly
```

---

#### Strategy 3: Original Loan Amount Analysis

**Best For:** Properties owned 20+ years

**Why It Matters:**
- If owned 20 years, original loan might be paid down significantly
- BUT if they refinanced recently, you can see the new loan amount
- Large recent loans = more rate sensitivity

**Caution:** Don't rely on "original loan" for 20+ year ownership because it doesn't show refinances.

**Better Approach:** Use **Equity vs. Value** calculation
```
Value - Equity = Current Loan Balance
$1,000,000 - $200,000 = $800,000 loan
```
