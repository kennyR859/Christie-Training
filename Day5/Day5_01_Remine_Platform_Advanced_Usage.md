## ðŸ’¡ Smart Filter Combinations

### Strategy 1: The ARM Bomb (High Probability Sellers)

**Goal:** Find homeowners whose mortgage payments are about to skyrocket

**Filters to Apply:**

```
Property Value: $1,000,000 minimum
Ownership Time: 20+ years
Loan Type: ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage)
Interest Rate: Under 4.5%
```

**Why This Works:**

**The Math:**
```
Current ARM Rate: 2.0%
Monthly Payment: $5,000

Reset Rate: 7.0%
New Monthly Payment: $10,000+

Result: Cannot afford doubled payment â†’ MUST SELL
```

**Your Approach:**
These homeowners are **highly motivated** but may not realize it yet. Your job is to contact them BEFORE the rate resets.

**Script:**
> "Hi, I'm calling neighbors in the area. I have buyers interested in your neighborhood. Do you know anyone thinking about selling?"

When they say "Actually, I've been considering it..." â†’ You've found gold.

### Strategy 2: The Equity Play

**Goal:** Find homeowners with enough equity to make a move

**Filters:**
```
Property Value: $800,000 minimum
Equity: $200,000 minimum (25%+)
Ownership Time: 10+ years
```

**Why This Works:**

**The Psychology:**

**Your Approach:**

**Your Message:**

### Strategy 3: The HELOC Signal

**Goal:** Find homeowners who pulled cash out (potential financial need)

**Filters:**
```

**Why This Works:**

**The Signal:**
A HELOC (second mortgage) indicates:
1. They needed cash recently
2. They chose NOT to sell (pulled HELOC instead)
3. They may be ready to sell now (tried to delay, but can't anymore)

**The Math:**
```

Equity: $250,000
â†’ Total Loans: $750,000 (75% LTV)
â†’ High debt load = rate-sensitive
```

**Your Approach:**
Soft, non-pressuring contact. They may be financially stressed.

**Script:**
> "Hi, I specialize in helping homeowners in [neighborhood] navigate their options. Whether you're staying or considering a move, I'd love to provide a free market analysis so you know where you stand. No obligation."

### Understanding Equity vs. Original Loan

**âš ï¸ Common Mistake:**

Using "Original Loan Amount" for long-term owners (20+ years).

**Why It's Wrong:**
```
Bought in 2000: Original loan $300,000
Refinanced in 2015: New loan $500,000
Refinanced in 2019: New loan $400,000

"Original Loan" shows $300,000 (WRONG)
Actual current loan: ~$380,000 (after paydown from 2019)
```

**Better Approach:**

Use **Equity Calculation:**
```
Property Value - Equity = Approximate Current Loan Balance

Example:
$1,000,000 value
- $200,000 equity
= $800,000 approximate loan balance
```

This is more accurate for long-term owners who've refinanced.

### The "Mortgage Age" Filter

**What It Tells You:**

How long ago they took out (or refinanced) their current mortgage.

**Use Case:**

**Find people who refinanced at LOW rates (now stuck):**

Property Value: $800,000+
```

**Why They're Stuck:**
- Locked in 2.5%-3.5% rates
- Today's rates are 7%+
- Moving means DOUBLING their rate
- BUT: Life changes force moves anyway (divorce, job, downsizing)

**Your Angle:**
Help them understand the **total cost** picture, not just the rate.

> "Yes, your rate will go up. But if you're moving to a smaller home or different area, your overall housing cost might still be LOWER. Let me show you the math."

## ðŸ“¤ Exporting Data Like a Pro

### Method 1: Select All (Full Farm Export)

**When to Use:**
You've filtered to exactly who you want, and you want everyone (under 10,000).

**Steps:**

1. **Apply all filters** (verify count is under 10,000)

2. **Click "Select All"** checkbox (top of results list)
   - All properties in current filter are selected
   - Counter shows: "2,340 selected" (or whatever your count is)

3. **Click "Cart" button**

4. **Name your cart**
   - Use descriptive names: `Ridgewood_ARM_Nov2024`
   - NOT generic names: `List1`, `Farm`

5. **Click "Create"**

6. **Click "Add [X] properties to cart"**
   - System adds all selected properties
   - May take 30-60 seconds for large batches

7. **Go to "Cart" tab**
   - Verify all properties are there
   - Check count matches

---

### Method 2: Individual Selection (Cherry-Picking)

**When to Use:**
You want specific properties, not everyone in the filter.

**Steps:**

1. **Browse filtered results**

2. **Click the cart icon** on each property you want
   - OR click the small circle checkbox (left side of each listing)

3. **Properties auto-add to cart** as you click

4. **Create cart name** when prompted (first property only)

5. **Continue adding** (they go to same cart)

**Use Case Example:**

Client says: "I love Chestnut Street. Are there any houses for sale there?"

â†’ Filter for Chestnut Street
â†’ Select only properties on that specific street
â†’ Add to cart: `ChestnutSt_Buyer_Smith`
â†’ Monitor for new listings (call owners directly)

---

### Method 3: Map-Based Selection

**When to Use:**
You want a specific geographic cluster visible on the map.

**Steps:**

1. **Zoom to desired area**

2. **Click the "select" tool** (lasso icon)

3. **Draw around properties** you want

4. **All properties in drawn area = selected**

5. **Add to cart** (same as Method 1)

**Best For:**
- Specific blocks
- Around a landmark (near train station)
- Custom radius around your listing

## ðŸš« Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Farm Too Large

**The Problem:**
```
Agent exports 5,000 properties
Tries to mail all 5,000
Cost: $7,500 for ONE mailing
Result: Cannot afford to follow up
```

**The Solution:**

Start with **100-300 properties** max.

**Better to:**
- Mail 200 people 10 times ($3,000)
- Than mail 2,000 people 1 time ($3,000)

**Rule of 20 Touches:**
You need **minimum 20 touches** to convert a cold lead.

---

### Mistake 2: Not Using Zip Code Search

**The Problem:**

Drawing on map in urban areas = **incomplete data** (especially buildings).

**The Solution:**

**Use ZIP CODE filter instead:**

```
Instead of: Drawing around Hoboken buildings
Try: Filter by zip code 07030
```

Result: More complete data, especially for condos/apartments.

---

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Mailing Address vs. Property Address

**The Scenario:**

Owner lives at different address than the property (absentee owner or investment property).

**Your Data Shows:**
```
Property Address: 123 Main St, Ridgewood, NJ
Mailing Address: 456 Oak Ave, Miami, FL
```

**What to Do:**

**For Mailers:** Use MAILING address (that's where they actually receive mail)

**For Property Analysis:** Use PROPERTY address

**In your export:**
- Both addresses should be in separate columns
- Choose the right one for your purpose

---

### Mistake 4: Mailing from Remine Directly

**Why It's Tempting:**

**Why You Shouldn't:**

3. âŒ **No company support** - If something breaks, you're on your own
4. âŒ **Not integrated with CRM** - Can't track who responded
5. âŒ **Limited templates** - Not optimized for luxury market

**Better Approach:**

### Mistake 5: Not Updating Your Farm

**The Problem:**

You export data in January. You call them in June using 5-month-old data.

**What Changed:**
- 3 properties already sold (you look foolish calling them)
- 2 phone numbers disconnected

**The Solution:**

**Re-export every 30-60 days** to refresh data.

Or better:

**Use Remine alerts:**
- Set alerts for your saved searches
- Get notified when properties in your farm:
  - Go on market
  - Sell
  - Have owner changes
  - Have financial changes (new loan, etc.)

### Strategy: The "Coming Soon" List

**Goal:** Find off-market properties to show buyers BEFORE MLS

**How:**

**Step 1: Build farm of motivated sellers** (using techniques above)

**Result:**
- Buyer is impressed (exclusive access)

## â“ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How much does Remine cost?

**A:** Typically included in your Christie's agent fees. Check with your manager.

### Q: Can I use Remine for properties outside New Jersey?

**A:** Yes! Remine covers most of the United States. Great for finding out-of-state buyer prospects.

### Q: How accurate is the equity/loan data?

**A:** Estimated accuracy is 80-85%. It's directional, not exact. Use it to identify potential, not as absolute truth.

### Q: Can I share my exported data with another agent?

**A:** Check your agreement. Generally, data is for YOUR use. Don't distribute to competitors.

### Q: How often is Remine data updated?

**A:** Varies by county. Generally 30-90 days lag from public records. Enough for farming, not for real-time transaction data.

### Q: What if a property shows "No phone number available"?

**A:** Common for privacy-conscious owners. You can:
- Mail them instead
- Try skip tracing service (additional cost)
- Visit in person (door knocking)
