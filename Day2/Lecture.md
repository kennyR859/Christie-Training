## Part 1: NAR Lawsuit & Agency Changes (Fern)

### Historical Context: How We Got Here

**Clear Cooperation Policy** (The Beginning of Changes)
- Problem: Some agents were withholding listings from MLS
- They were using everyone else's listings but keeping their own private
- **Solution:** Clear Cooperation rule - Must post to MLS within 24 hours

**Three ways to comply:**
1. **Office Exclusive** - Stage and clean, then release
2. **Non-dissemination** - Do necessary prep, then go public
3. **Delayed showing** - List today, show at open house (sales technique)

**The Fatal Mistake**
> The NAR Board of Directors said: "Sellers must pay buyer's agents too."

**Department of Justice Response:**
- Saw this as price fixing
- Major lawsuit resulted
- Multi-million dollar settlement
- Changed the entire industry

---

### The Settlement Terms

**What Changed:**
1. **No commission shown in MLS** - It's now just information dissemination
2. **Mandatory buyer agency agreements** - Before showing properties
3. **Seller's property disclosure** - Must be uploaded with listing
4. **Lead paint disclosure** - Must be uploaded within 24 hours

**New Jersey Governor's Additional Rules:**
- Wanted his own requirements added
- Even stricter than NAR settlement
- Added open house restrictions
- Hired 33 new investigators for Northern NJ

### Agency Relationships Explained

#### Sub-Agency (The Old Way - DEAD)
**How it worked:**
- Everyone worked for the seller
- Buyer's agent was "sub-agent" of seller
- Buyer told their agent information
- Agent had to tell seller everything

**Why it died:**
- Buyer had no true representation
- Created conflicts of interest
- Not fair to buyers

**Example that created the law:**
> Congressman's daughter getting married, buying house with husband. They said: "We'll offer $500K but if we have to, we'll go to $550K."
> Agent went to seller and said: "They're offering $500K but will pay $550K."
> Seller took $550K.
> Later at neighborhood party, they learned what happened.
> Congressman was furious â†’ New laws created

#### Buyer Agency (Current Standard)
**What it means:**
- Agent works exclusively for buyer
- Buyer's interests come first
- Confidentiality protected
- Must disclose only "things of substance" to seller

**Things of substance:**
- Mechanical issues
- Structural problems
- Environmental concerns

**What you CANNOT disclose:**
- Buyer is getting divorced
- Buyer inherited $2 million
- Buyer will pay more if needed
- Personal financial information

#### Dual Agency
**Definition:** Agent represents both buyer and seller

**How it happens:**
- Your office listing + your buyer
- Same company, different agents can create dual agency

**The problem:**
- "Who do you really represent?"
- Buyers feel: "You'll tell seller everything"
- Creates distrust

**Best practice:**
> Use **Designated Agency** instead

#### Designated Agency (Preferred Solution)
**How it works:**
- Listing agent represents seller
- Different agent in office represents buyer
- Each has clear loyalty
- Avoids dual agency problems

**Quote from instructor:**
> "Designated agency has been done by Christie's for 40 years. We just did it. Now it's in the law, so what we're doing is legal."

**When you must use it:**
- Any Christie's listing + your buyer = Dual agency situation
- **Solution:** Designate another agent to represent buyer
- Or have manager oversee if you handle both sides

### Buyer's Agreement Requirements

#### What It Is
**Purpose:** Written contract between agent and buyer before showing properties

**Key components:**
1. Who you represent (buyer agent, seller agent, dual agent checkbox)
2. Does buyer have relationship with another broker?
3. Term of agreement (how long it lasts)
4. Commission arrangement

#### Best Practices

**For Third-Party Referrals (Upcity, out-of-area buyers):**
> Start with **1-week agreement**

**Script:**
"Let's just do this for one week while you're here looking. We can always extend it to a full transaction. If it doesn't work out, we can terminate with written notice."

**Why this works:**
- Less threatening for new relationships
- Buyer feels in control
- You prove your value
- Easy to extend

**Important reminder:**
> "You can show them EVERYTHING in the state of New Jersey. They don't need to go to 10 different brokers."

### Commission Compensation Strategy

#### The New Reality
**What changed:**
- Listing agent no longer dictates your compensation
- YOU set your compensation in the offer
- Seller decides whether to pay it

#### Recommended Approach
> **Request 3% on all offers**

**Why 3%:**
- You can always go down, can't go up
- In multiple offers, highest NET to seller often wins
- If your offer nets seller the most, they'll pay your 3%

**Real example from instructor:**
> "I listed at 6% (old school full fee), offering 2.5% to buyer's agent. Buyer's agent only had 2% on their agreement. I got 4% commission. I was like 'yay!'"

**New agent success story:**
> "Half of my agents came back after I told them to ask for 3%. They got it! The seller wanted the most money and was willing to pay the agent who brought the best offer."

#### The Math
**Scenario:** House listed at $700K, market value ~$800K (hot market)

**Offer A:** $800K + 2% buyer's agent commission
**Offer B:** $825K + 3% buyer's agent commission

**Seller's net:**
- Offer A: $800K - $16K = $784K net
- Offer B: $825K - $24.75K = $800.25K net

**Result:** Seller takes Offer B and pays the 3%

**Quote:**
> "Agents are getting raises now because you ask for what you're worth. The highest offer that nets the seller the most - they want that buyer and will pay that agent."

### Open House Procedures (NJ Law)

#### What You CAN Do
- Show the property
- Represent the seller ONLY
- Display required disclosure signage
- Provide property information

#### What You CANNOT Do
- Discuss offers during open house hours
- Sign buyer agency agreements
- Write contracts
- Conduct ANY transaction business

#### Required Signage
**Must display:**
- Disclosure stating you represent the seller only
- Available in Platform for download
- Post prominently (recommended: front door)

**Investigators WILL:**
- Show up at open houses
- Photograph your signage
- Test you with questions
- Create dossier on your compliance

**Penalties:**
- License suspension
- Fines up to $15,000

#### After Open House
**If someone wants to make offer:**

**Option 1:** Close open house (end time), close door to public, write contract

**Option 2:** Make appointment for after open house ends

**Option 3:** Have partner/buddy agent handle buyer while you finish open house

**Critical timing rule:**
> If open house ends at 3pm but someone walks in at 2:59pm, the open house is still open until they leave. Close door, meet elsewhere, or wait.

#### Open House Exception
**For the specific open house property:**
- Buyer wants to purchase that house = No buyer agency agreement needed
- You're working for seller at open house

**For other properties:**
- Buyer wants to see different houses = Buyer agency agreement required

### Circle Prospecting Deep Dive

#### The Numbers
**NAR Statistics:**
> "When one house sells in a neighborhood, 3 others come on market within 1-1.5 years."

**Success rate:**

- Leave open house invitation

**Methods:**
1. Door knocking (in person)

### Farming Strategy

#### What is Farming?
**Definition:** Pick a specific area and market consistently to it

**Examples:**
- High-rise building
- Neighborhood of 200-300 homes
- Specific zip code
- Condo complex

#### How to Choose a Farm
> **CRITICAL:** Check the turnover rate first!

**Example - Don't farm here:**
- Midland Park: Houses pass generation to generation
- Very low inventory
- Waste of time and money

**Example - Good for farming:**
- Fairlawn: ~400 houses sell per year
- Certain high-rises with high turnover
- Areas with frequent moves

#### Farming ROI
- More expensive than circle prospecting
- Results take 6-12 months minimum
- Requires consistent monthly touches
- Can be very profitable long-term

**Recommendation:**
> For new agents: Start with Sphere & Circle. Add farming after you have income.

### Personal Notes

#### Why Handwritten Notes Work
**Statistics:**
- People remember who sent them
- Feel special and valued
- Message: "I remember you, I like you, I care for you"
- More personal than email

#### Best Card Design Story
> Agent had simple card: First and last initial with key between them on front. Inside was blank for note. Back had her contact info.
>
> She said: "All I wanted was for them to remember my name."
>
> **Result:** Does 7-10 million per year consistently.

#### When to Send Notes
- After showing properties
- After open house visits
- After listing presentations (even if you didn't get it)
- Birthdays/Anniversaries
- Holidays
- "Just thinking of you" check-ins
- Congratulations (promotion, new baby, etc.)

### Communication Hierarchy

#### 1. Phone Calls (Highest Priority)
**For:** Active clients during transactions

**Why:**
- Personal connection
- Immediate answers
- Hand-holding through process
- Builds trust

**Warning story:**
> "Agent said 'I don't answer my phone anymore, I only text.' I said 'You're risking your career.' She lost it. Clients thought she went out of business."

**Best practice:**
> Ask each client: "How do you want me to communicate with you and how often?"

**Example:**
> "One guy called and said 'Stop it, it's too much.' I asked 'How often works for you?' He said 'Once a week.' I switched to once a week, he was happy."

#### 2. Email (Documentation)
**For:**
- Storing documents
- Tracking showings
- Confirmations
- Property information

**Showing confirmation example:**
> "This is what we saw today: Properties A, B, C, D. What did you think?"
>
> Reply creates record that you showed those properties.

**When Google was breached:**
> "I was having a stroke because I couldn't get to my documents. Everything's in email."

**Organization:**
> Use Outlook folders to organize by client, property, transaction type.

#### 3. Text Messages (Quick Items Only)
**Appropriate for:**
- "I'm late, be there in 15 minutes"
- "What are the directions?"
- Quick confirmations
- Scheduling

**Not appropriate for:**
- Complex explanations
- Emotional conversations
- Contract discussions
- Important decisions

### Know Your Inventory

#### Why It Matters
**Scenario:**
> Listing presentation, sellers ask about house down the street. If you don't know it, they lose confidence. "3 strikes you're out."

#### How to Stay Current
**Daily morning routine:**
1. Check what sold in your area
2. Check new listings
3. Review price changes
4. Note open houses

**Before appointments:**
1. Review MLS for that town
2. Last 90 days: Sales, under contract, active
3. Drive the neighborhood
4. Note competing properties

**Easy in low inventory markets:**
> "Ridgewood used to have 135-140 houses for sale. Now there's 14. That's easy to know!"

#### Go on Inspections
- Attend open houses
- Tour new listings
- Learn your market
- See what's selling
