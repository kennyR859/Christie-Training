# Day 10 - Topic 06: Realist Property Research Platform

## Overview

Realist is a comprehensive property research platform that provides detailed information beyond standard MLS data, serving as an essential tool for real estate professionals conducting pre-listing research, buyer analysis, and market evaluations. Understanding how to leverage Realist effectively enhances agent professionalism, improves client service, and provides competitive advantage through superior market knowledge.

**Key Learning Objectives:**
- Understand what Realist is and how it differs from MLS
- Access and navigate the Realist platform
- Identify what property information is available
- Know when to use Realist vs. other research tools
- Integrate Realist into pre-listing and buyer research workflows
- Maximize value from enhanced property data

---

## What is Realist?

### Platform Definition

**Realist** is a comprehensive property research platform that aggregates and presents detailed property information from multiple authoritative sources including:
- County tax assessor records
- Municipal property records
- MLS historical data
- Public record databases
- Geographic information systems (GIS)
- Property ownership history
- Sales transaction history

### Purpose in Real Estate

**Primary Functions:**
1. **Pre-Listing Research** - Gather complete property information before listing appointments
2. **Buyer Property Investigation** - Deep dive into properties before making offers
3. **Comparative Market Analysis** - Enhanced data for pricing strategies
4. **Investment Property Evaluation** - Historical data and neighborhood analytics
5. **Title and Ownership Research** - Verify ownership and property characteristics

### How Realist Differs from MLS

**MLS (Multiple Listing Service):**
- Current listings for sale
- Active, pending, sold properties
- Agent-entered information
- Marketing photos and descriptions
- Commission structures
- Showing instructions

**Realist:**
- Tax assessment data (all properties, not just listed)
- Historical ownership records
- Detailed property characteristics from tax records
- Sometimes includes surveys and plats
- Aerial imagery and maps
- Neighborhood demographics
- Market trends and analytics
- School district boundaries
- Comparable sales beyond MLS timeframes

**Complementary Tools:**
Both platforms serve different purposes and should be used together for comprehensive property research.

## Information Available in Realist

### Property Tax and Assessment Data

**Tax Assessment Information:**
- Current annual tax amount (municipal, county, school breakdown)
- Total assessment value
- Land assessment value
- Improvement (building) assessment value
- Assessment history over time
- Tax payment status
- Special assessments or liens

**Why Tax Data Matters:**

**For Listing Agents:**
- Accurate tax information for MLS listing
- Identify assessment increases that may affect marketability
- Explain property value trends to sellers
- Compare assessments to market values

**For Buyers:**
- Understand ongoing carrying costs
- Compare tax burden to similar properties
- Identify potential tax appeals
- Budget accurately for homeownership

**Assessment vs. Market Value:**
Important to understand that assessed value ≠ market value. In New Jersey and many states, properties assessed at percentage of market value with periodic revaluations.

### Property Characteristics

**Building Details:**
- Square footage (living area)
- Year built
- Number of stories
- Basement (finished/unfinished square footage)
- Garage (attached/detached, number of cars)
- Number of rooms by type
- Bathrooms (full/half)
- Bedrooms
- Construction type (frame, brick, stone)
- Roof type and material
- Heating system type
- Cooling system (central air, none, window units)

**Lot Information:**
- Total lot square footage
- Lot dimensions (sometimes)
- **Frontage** (sometimes available when not in MLS tax records)
- Lot shape and topography
- Zoning classification
- Flood zone designation

**Unique Features:**
- Fireplace quantity and type
- Pool (in-ground/above-ground)
- Deck/patio square footage
- Outbuildings (shed, barn)
- Special features noted in tax records

### Property Ownership History

**Ownership Records:**
- Current owner name(s)
- Purchase date
- Purchase price (if disclosed)
- Length of ownership
- Previous owners
- Ownership transfers over time

**Sales History:**
- Sale dates going back decades
- Sale prices (when recorded)
- Transfer type (sale, inheritance, gift)
- Frequency of turnover
- Price appreciation over time

**Use Cases:**

**For Listing Agents:**
- Verify seller's purchase price and date
- Calculate appreciation since purchase
- Identify long-term owners (may have deferred maintenance)
- Understand property's market history

**For Buyers:**
- Identify frequent flips (potential red flag)
- Verify seller's holding period
- Research price history for negotiation insights
- Understand property's value trajectory

### School District Information

**School Assignment Data:**
- Elementary school boundary
- Middle school assignment
- High school district
- School district name
- Links to school websites
- District contact information

**Important Caution:**

**Instructor's Warning:**
> "The schools can change. In Ridgewood for instance, this year you have probably 25 new kids going to Ridge School and next year there's 50, so they may divide a street off and put it to Orchard or something. They can move it all around."

**Proper Use:**
- Provide current school assignment
- Include disclaimer: "School boundaries subject to change"
- Direct buyers: "Verify with school district office"
- Never guarantee school assignments
- Avoid rating or comparing school quality (fair housing)

### Survey Information (Sometimes Available)

**Survey Documents in Realist:**

**Instructor's Note:**
> "Realist—sometimes realist even has a survey."

**When Surveys Available:**
- Some municipalities upload surveys to public records
- Recent sales may have survey attached
- Subdivision plats and site plans
- Property boundary maps
- Easement locations

**Value of Survey Access:**
- Verify lot dimensions without seller providing survey
- Identify easements for listing preparation
- Calculate frontage when not available elsewhere
- Understand property boundaries
- Prepare for listing questions

**Limitation:**
Not all properties have surveys in Realist. If critical information needed, still request survey from seller or order new survey.

### Aerial Imagery and Maps

**Visual Property Analysis:**
- Current aerial/satellite photos
- Historical aerial imagery (see property changes over time)
- Street view integration
- Topographic maps
- Parcel boundary overlays
- Flood zone maps
- Zoning maps

**Use Cases:**

**Property Changes Over Time:**
- Compare current aerial to historical
- Identify additions to home
- Verify pool installation date
- See landscape changes
- Detect unpermitted structures

**Neighborhood Context:**
- Proximity to commercial areas
- Distance to schools, parks, amenities
- Traffic patterns and major roads
- Neighboring property characteristics
- Environmental features (wetlands, streams)

### Neighborhood Demographics and Analytics

**Demographic Information:**
- Population density
- Age distribution
- Income levels
- Education levels
- Household composition
- Employment statistics

**Market Trends:**
- Average days on market
- Sale price to list price ratios
- Inventory levels
- Price trends over time
- Comparable sales density
- Market velocity

**Use for CMAs:**
Enhanced comparable market analysis with:
- Deeper historical context
- Broader geographic data
- Trend identification
- Market condition assessment

### Buyer Property Research

**Before Making Offers:**

**Property Investigation:**
1. **Ownership Verification**

2. **Tax and Assessment Review**
   - Understand ongoing tax burden

   - Identify special assessments

3. **Property Characteristics Verification**
   - Cross-check MLS listing accuracy

4. **School Research**

## Best Practices for Using Realist

### Pre-Listing Appointment Research

**Standard Operating Procedure:**

**1. Property Lookup (5 minutes)**
- Enter property address in Realist
- Generate comprehensive property report
- Review all sections for accuracy and completeness

**2. Tax Assessment Review (3 minutes)**
- Note total annual taxes
- Review assessment breakdown
- Compare assessment to market value expectations
- Identify recent changes or appeals

**3. Property Characteristics Verification (3 minutes)**
- Confirm square footage (living area)
- Verify lot size and dimensions
- Check bedroom/bathroom count
- Note special features (pool, garage, fireplace)

**4. Ownership and Sales History (2 minutes)**
- Seller's purchase date and price
- Calculate holding period
- Review previous sales
- Identify any red flags

**5. School Research (2 minutes)**
- Confirm school assignments
- Access school website links
- Prepare school information handout
- Include disclaimer about potential changes

**6. Print and Organize (2 minutes)**
- Print comprehensive report
- Highlight key information
- Organize in listing appointment folder
- Bring to appointment for reference

**Total Time Investment: 15-20 minutes**
**Return: Professional credibility, informed discussion, confident presentation**

### Documentation and File Management

**Realist Report Filing:**

**For Every Listing:**
- Print Realist property report
- Include in listing preparation file
- Save digital copy to property folder
- Reference during pricing discussion
- Use for MLS data accuracy verification

**For Buyer Clients:**
- Generate reports for properties under consideration
- Include in buyer's property comparison file
- Save for due diligence documentation
- Reference during offer strategy discussion

**Retention:**
- Keep Realist reports for transaction duration
- Maintain in closed file for liability protection
- Documents research performed
- Proves professional due diligence

### Data Verification

**Cross-Reference Critical Information:**

**Never Rely on Single Source:**
- Compare Realist square footage to MLS listing
- Verify tax amount with seller's tax bill
- Confirm lot size with survey when available
- Cross-check ownership with seller's deed

**When Discrepancies Found:**
- Note discrepancy in file
- Investigate which source is accurate
- Resolve before listing or making offer
- Document resolution

**Trust But Verify:**
- Realist aggregates from multiple sources
- Data entry errors possible
- Municipal records sometimes outdated
- Always verify critical facts with primary source documents

### Compliance and Fair Housing

**School Information Handling:**

**Provide Factual Data Only:**
- ✅ "According to Realist, the assigned elementary school is [Name]"
- ✅ "Here's the school website where you can find more information"
- ✅ "School assignments can change; verify with district office"
- ❌ "This is the best school in the district"
- ❌ "You want to be in this school, not that one"
- ❌ Comparisons or ratings of schools

**After Buyer Mentions Children:**
Once buyer brings up their children, you may:
- Discuss age-appropriate programs
- Connect with parents of similar-age children
- Provide contact information for schools
- Share extracurricular and sports information
- Still no comparative quality statements

**Document School Discussions:**
- Note what factual information provided
- Record that disclaimer given
- Keep copy of information provided
- Protect against fair housing claims

## Real-World Applications

### Scenario 1: Pre-Listing Discovery

**Situation:**
Agent preparing for listing appointment on 1985 colonial.

**Realist Research Reveals:**
- Property purchased by seller in 2005 for $425,000
- Current assessment: $650,000
- Tax records show 4 bedrooms, 2.5 baths
- Lot: 0.45 acres (19,602 square feet)
- Aerial imagery shows pool added circa 2010 (not in original sale)

**Agent Preparation:**
- Calculates seller's appreciation: $425K purchase (potential basis for pricing discussion)
- Notes pool addition (verify if permitted, affects value)
- Confirms lot size for marketing
- Prepares CMA based on appreciation since 2005

**Listing Appointment Success:**
- Seller impressed with agent's knowledge
- Pool addition discussed (seller confirms permitted)
- Pricing conversation informed by purchase price
- Professional presentation builds trust

### Scenario 2: Buyer Due Diligence

**Situation:**
Buyer interested in property listed at $725,000.

**Realist Research Reveals:**
- Current owner purchased 18 months ago for $525,000
- Property listed 6 months ago at $795,000, reduced to $725,000
- Tax assessment: $480,000 (seems low compared to asking)
- Aerial shows addition to rear of home (circa 2020)

**Red Flags:**
- Quick flip (18 months)
- Large price increase attempt ($525K to $795K = 51% markup)
- Assessment much lower than asking price

**Agent Investigation:**
- Research permits for addition (was it permitted?)
- Calculate flip markup ($200K profit attempt)
- Compare to legitimate comps (is $725K realistic?)
- Advise buyer on potential issues

**Outcome:**
- Buyer makes lower offer based on research
- Negotiates knowing seller's recent purchase price
- Includes inspection contingency given quick flip
- Avoids overpaying for unpermitted addition

### Scenario 3: School Verification

**Situation:**
Buyer family with elementary-age children considering two properties.

**Realist Research Shows:**
- Property A: Ridge Elementary assignment
- Property B: Orchard Elementary assignment
- Both in same town (Ridgewood example)

**Agent Action:**
- Provide Realist school information printout
- Include links to both school websites
- Provide school district contact information
- Recommend parents contact schools directly

**Fair Housing Compliance:**
- ✅ Provides factual assignment information
- ✅ Gives equal information about both schools
- ✅ Directs to official sources for verification
- ❌ Does NOT compare school quality
- ❌ Does NOT recommend one over other

**Buyer Decision:**
- Reviews both school websites
- Contacts schools for tours
- Makes informed decision based on own research
- Agent provided tools, not opinions

## Common Questions and Troubleshooting

### "Is Realist Information Always Accurate?"

**Answer:**
Realist aggregates data from multiple authoritative sources, but data entry errors and outdated information possible.

**Best Practice:**
- Use Realist as starting point
- Verify critical information with primary sources
- Cross-reference with MLS, surveys, deeds
- Confirm with seller or municipal offices when necessary

### "What If Survey Not Available in Realist?"

**Answer:**
Not all properties have surveys uploaded to public records.

**Alternatives:**
- Request survey from seller
- Check with municipality (sometimes have surveys on file)
- Order new survey if critical for transaction
- Use Realist lot dimensions (when available) as estimate

### "How Current is the Data?"

**Answer:**
Varies by data type and municipality:
- Tax assessments: Updated annually or when reassessment occurs
- Sales data: Updated as recorded with county (typically within weeks)
- Property characteristics: Updated when municipality updates tax records
- Ownership: Updated as deeds recorded

**When Currency Critical:**
Contact municipality directly for most current information.

### "Can I Share Realist Reports with Clients?"

**Answer:**
Yes, appropriate to share property information with clients.

**Best Practices:**
- Print relevant sections for clients
- Explain source and nature of information
- Include disclaimers (especially for schools)
- Note that information subject to verification
- Document what information provided

### "What If Realist and MLS Differ?"

**Answer:**
Investigate discrepancy to determine accurate information.

**Common Discrepancies:**
- Square footage (different measurement methods)
- Year built (tax record vs. actual construction)
- Lot size (survey vs. tax record estimate)
- Features (MLS updated, tax records lag)

**Resolution:**
- Favor primary source documents (survey, deed, plans)
- Ask seller for clarification
- Update MLS if MLS incorrect
- Note discrepancy in file
