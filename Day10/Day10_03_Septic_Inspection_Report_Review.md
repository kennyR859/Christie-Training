## Septic Inspection vs Home Inspection

### Critical Distinction

**Two Separate Services:**
- **Home inspection companies** ≠ **Septic inspection companies**
- Completely different specializations
- Require different certifications and expertise
- Cannot substitute one for the other

**Home Inspectors:**
- Inspect structure, systems, appliances
- General assessment of property condition
- MAY note septic system exists
- NOT qualified to assess septic system condition

**Septic Inspectors:**
- Specialized septic system certification
- Pump and inspect tank and distribution system
- Test system functionality
- Provide detailed technical report

**Agent Responsibility:**
- Ensure buyer contracts specify **septic inspection** required
- Don't assume home inspector covers septic
- Recommend reputable septic inspection companies
- Budget separately for septic inspection ($300-600 typical)

## Repair vs. Replacement Decision

### The 9 Out of 10 Rule

> "If it's determined that a repair is needed, nine times out of 10 you're not going to need an engineer. You'll just need a septic company to make that repair, and we do have people that we can refer you to."
> — Sherry, MAP Engineering

**Repair Scenarios (Most Common):**
- Clogged distribution box (D-box)
- Broken baffle wall in tank
- Damaged effluent filter
- Cracked tank lid
- Failed pump in ATU system
- Saturated laterals (may be temporary)

**Cost Range for Repairs:**
- Minor: $500-1,500 (filter, baffle, lid)
- Moderate: $1,500-5,000 (D-box, pump, partial lateral)
- Major repair: $5,000-10,000 (extensive lateral work)

**Who Handles Repairs:**
- Septic service/maintenance companies
- NOT engineers (no design needed)
- MAP can refer to trusted repair companies
- Faster and much cheaper than replacement

---

### Replacement/Alteration Scenarios (Less Common)

**When Full Replacement Needed:**

1. **Complete System Failure:**
   - Leach field completely saturated and non-functional
   - Tank structurally compromised beyond repair
   - System not meeting current code requirements

2. **Septic Alteration Required:**
   - "Alteration" = **whole new system** (same as replacement)
   - Health department deems system non-compliant
   - Soil conditions no longer support existing design
   - Bedroom count increase requires larger system

**Cost Range for Replacement:**
- Small system (2-3 bedroom): $25,000-30,000
- Medium system (4 bedroom): $30,000-40,000
- Large system (5+ bedroom): $35,000-50,000+

**Who Handles Replacement:**
- Engineers required (MAP Engineering)
- Full design and health department approval process
- Licensed installation contractors
- 10-16 week timeline

## Common Report Terms Decoded

### Tank and Components

**Baffle Walls:**
- Internal walls in septic tank
- Prevent solids from entering distribution system
- **If damaged:** Repair ($800-1,500)
- **NOT replacement territory**

**Effluent Filter:**
- Filter at tank outlet
- Prevents solids from clogging leach field
- **If clogged/damaged:** Clean or replace ($300-800)
- **Simple maintenance item**

**Distribution Box (D-box):**
- Distributes wastewater evenly to leach field laterals
- **If clogged:** Clean out ($500-1,000)
- **If cracked:** Replace ($1,500-3,000)
- **Still NOT full replacement**

---

### Leach Field Issues

**Laterals:**
- Perforated pipes in leach field
- Distribute wastewater into soil
- **If clogged:** May be cleanable or need partial replacement
- **If saturated:** Could be temporary (high water table) or permanent failure

**Soil Saturation:**
- Ground around leach field is waterlogged
- **Temporary causes:** Heavy rain, high water table, seasonal
- **Permanent failure:** Soil biomat clogged, system at end of life

**Field Failure:**
- Complete inability of soil to accept wastewater
- **Symptoms:** Sewage backup, standing water, odors
- **This = replacement territory**

---

### System Types

**Conventional System:**
- Gravity-based distribution
- No moving parts
- **Most common type**
- **Easiest to maintain**

**ATU (Advanced Treatment Unit):**
- Mechanical aeration
- Requires electricity
- **Maintenance:** Twice yearly filter cleaning
- **If pump fails:** Replace pump ($1,500-3,000), not entire system

**Gray Water System:**
- Separate system for non-toilet wastewater
- Sinks, showers, laundry
- **If failing:** May reconnect to main septic (repair, not replacement)

## When Report Says "Unsatisfactory"

### What "Unsatisfactory" Actually Means

**Report Language:** "System is in unsatisfactory condition"

**What Agent/Client Hears:** "You need a new septic system for $40,000"

**What It Often ACTUALLY Means:**
- Some component needs attention
- Could be minor repair
- Could be deferred maintenance
- May not even need engineer

**MAP Engineer's Role:**
- Translate "unsatisfactory" into specific problem
- Determine if repair or replacement
- Provide cost range
