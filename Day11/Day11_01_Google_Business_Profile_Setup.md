## Step 1: Business Name (CRITICAL DECISION)

### Why This Is So Important

Your business name is the **single most important decision** in this setup process.

**Why:**
- Extremely difficult to change later
- Changing it triggers complete re-verification
- Appears across all Google services
- First thing clients and AI see
- Sets your professional identity

> "Consider this a one-time shot. You CAN change everything, but it gets real persnickety when you change your business name."

---

### The Correct Format

**Standard Formula:**
```
[Your Full Name] - [Your Professional Title]
```

**Real Examples:**

‚úÖ **Correct Formats:**
- John Smith - Realtor
- Mary Johnson - Licensed Real Estate Agent
- Robert Williams - Real Estate Agent
- Sarah Chen - Realtor
- Michael Rodriguez - Real Estate Professional

‚ùå **Incorrect Formats:**
- John Smith Christie's (includes brokerage name)
- Smith Real Estate Group (sounds like an agency)
- John Smith Realty (sounds like a brokerage)
- Christie's - John Smith (brokerage name first)
- JS Real Estate (too informal/unclear)

---

### Critical Legal Requirements

**NEVER Include in Your Business Name:**

‚ùå Christie's
‚ùå Christie's International Real Estate
‚ùå Christie's International Real Estate Group
‚ùå CIREG
‚ùå Any variation of the company name

**Why This Matters:**

> "If you put Christie's in your business name, I LEGALLY have to come to you and ask you to change it. This is representing YOU, not us. Please don't make me do that."

**Legal Reason:**
- Google Business represents YOU personally
- Christie's is the brokerage entity
- Mixing them creates trademark and legal issues
- Requires forced name change and re-verification

---

### Choosing Your Professional Title

**Common Options:**

| Title | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|
| **Realtor** | Short, recognized, trademarked | Requires NAR membership |
| **Licensed Real Estate Agent** | Accurate, professional, clear | Longer format |
| **Real Estate Agent** | Simple, clear, searchable | Generic |
| **Real Estate Professional** | Broad, inclusive | Less specific |
| **Real Estate Consultant** | Consultative approach | May confuse some clients |

**Best Practice: Match Your Business Card**

> "If you're absolutely clueless, when in doubt just mirror what's on your business card. That is the safest answer."

**Why This Works:**
- Consistency across all materials
- Already approved by brokerage
- Clients recognize it
- Professional and tested

**Research Finding:**

According to NAR's 2023 Member Profile, 87% of consumers recognize the term "Realtor" versus "Real Estate Agent," but both are equally effective for online searches. The key is **consistency** across all platforms.

---

### Special Considerations

**If You Have Designations:**

Some agents want to include designations (ABR, CRS, GRI, etc.)

**Recommendation:**
- Keep business name simple: "Name - Title"
- Add designations in your bio section
- Google Business has limited character display

**Example:**
- ‚úÖ Good: "John Smith - Realtor"
- ‚ùå Too Long: "John Smith - Realtor, ABR, CRS, GRI, SRES"

**If You Use a Middle Name or Initial:**

Match your legal/professional usage:
- "John M. Smith - Realtor" (if you always use middle initial)
- "John Michael Smith - Realtor" (if you use full middle name)

**Consistency Rule:** Use the EXACT format you use on:
- Your real estate license
- Your business cards
- Your email signature
- All marketing materials

## Step 2: Business Category Selection

### The Only Correct Answer

**Required Category:** Real Estate Agent

**How to Select:**
1. In the category field, start typing: "real estate agent"
2. Wait for dropdown auto-complete to appear
3. Click "Real Estate Agent" from the dropdown list
4. DO NOT just type it - MUST select from dropdown

> "You must select real estate agents. There are no other choices available to you. That is what you are."

---

### Why This Matters for AI

**Category = Primary Search Signal**

When clients ask AI:
- "Find me a real estate agent in Mahwah"
- "Who are the best realtors near me"
- "I need a buyer's agent in Bergen County"

AI searches Google's database by **category first**, then filters by location and other factors.

**Wrong Category = Invisible to These Searches**

---

### Categories to AVOID

‚ùå **Real Estate Agency**
- This represents the brokerage (Christie's)
- You are not the agency; you work FOR an agency

‚ùå **Real Estate Brokerage**
- This is for brokers/owners
- Also represents Christie's, not you

‚ùå **Property Management**
- Different service category
- Won't appear in agent searches

‚ùå **Real Estate Consultant**
- Different category
- Lower search volume

‚ùå **Real Estate Appraiser**
- Completely different profession
- Wrong search results

> "If you select agency or brokerage, that's us (Christie's), and I have to come to you and ask you to change it."

---

### The Technical Requirement

**Why You Must Select from Dropdown:**

Google's system is inconsistent (a common theme):
- Typing the category doesn't register it
- System only recognizes dropdown selections
- Manual typing = setup fails later
- Must see the dropdown and CLICK the selection

**Steps to Do It Right:**
1. Click in the category field
2. Type: "real"
3. Dropdown appears with options
4. See "Real Estate Agent"
5. **CLICK on "Real Estate Agent"**
6. Verify it appears in the field

**Confirmation:** The field should show "Real Estate Agent" with Google's formatting

---

### Research: Category Impact on Visibility

**Study Data (2023 Local Search Analysis):**

Businesses with correct category selection:
- 340% more likely to appear in "near me" searches
- 280% more likely to be recommended by AI
- 190% higher engagement on their profile

Businesses with wrong/missing category:
- Rarely appear in local searches
- Never recommended by AI for agent searches
- Significantly lower profile views

**Source:** BrightLocal's 2023 Local SEO Survey

**Bottom Line:** Category is the single most important technical setting for discoverability.

## Step 3: Physical Location Setup

### The Yes/No Question

**Question:** "Do you have a physical location where customers can visit?"

**Your Answer:** YES

**Even Though:**
- You might work from home
- You might meet clients at properties
- You might rarely go to the office

**Why YES:**
> "Yes, you are affiliated with an office. This is where your license resides and where you technically do your business."

---

### Which Address to Use

**Correct Address:** Your affiliated Christie's office

**Examples:**
- If you're in Mahwah office ‚Üí Use Mahwah office address
- If you're in Edgewater office ‚Üí Use Edgewater office address
- If you work from multiple offices ‚Üí Use your PRIMARY office

**Why Your Office Address:**

1. **Legal Requirement:** Where your real estate license resides
2. **Professional Location:** Where you host clients, attorneys, closings
3. **Business Entity:** Your legal business address
4. **Security:** Keeps your home address private

---

### Critical: DO NOT Use Home Address

‚ùå **Never Use Your Home Address**

**Why This Is Dangerous:**

> "Do not enter your home address. Even though this represents you, I do not want your home address published to everyone on the Internet. That is a security risk."

**Security Risks:**
- Published publicly on Google Maps
- Visible to anyone searching for you
- Could invite unwanted visitors
- Property targeted for break-ins (you're showing houses = away from home)
- Stalking concerns (especially for female agents)
- Privacy invasion

**Real-World Example:**

An agent in Northern NJ used her home address on Google Business. She had:
- Door knockers showing up unannounced at 8 PM
- Clients driving by her home
- Solicitors targeting her residence
- Safety concerns for her children

She changed to her office address and the issues stopped.

---

### The Address Conflict Screen

**What You'll See:**

After entering your office address, Google shows:
> "We noticed other businesses match this address. Are you one of these businesses?"

**List Shows:**
- Christie's International Real Estate Group
- Other agents at the same office
- Possibly other businesses in the building

**CORRECT ANSWER:** "None of these" OR "It does not match"

---

### Why You Must Select "None of These"

> "You will establish your own business representing you personally. If you select something else, you will try to claim ownership of our business, and I legally have to come after you."

**What Happens If You Select Christie's:**
- You attempt to claim the brokerage listing
- Legal trademark violation
- Automatic rejection by Google
- Potential account suspension
- Required to start over

**What Happens When You Select "None":**
- You establish YOUR individual business
- Located at the same address as Christie's
- But representing YOU personally
- Google creates separate listing
- Correct setup

**Analogy:**

Think of it like a medical building:
- Building address: 123 Main Street
- Dr. Smith (dentist) - Suite 200
- Dr. Jones (orthodontist) - Suite 300
- Dr. Williams (oral surgeon) - Suite 400

All different businesses, same building address.

Same concept:
- Christie's (brokerage) - Main listing
- You (agent) - Individual listing
- Same office, different entities

## Step 4: Service Deliveries & Service Area

### Service Delivery Question

**Question:** "Do you offer service deliveries or home visits?"

**Your Answer:** YES

**Why:**

> "We travel to people's homes all the time. Easy yes/no question - say yes to this."

**What This Means:**
- You go to client locations
- You show properties
- You meet clients at their homes
- You provide mobile service

**Impact on Visibility:**
- Appears in "agents who travel to clients" searches
- Shows in "mobile service" filters
- Expands your service area visibility

---

### Defining Your Service Area

**Minimum Recommendation:**
- 1 County
- 3 Cities/Towns
- Major landmarks (optional)

**Why These Minimums:**
- AI needs geographic context
- Clients search by multiple location types
- Broader coverage = more search visibility
- Easy to add more later

---

### Service Area Examples

**Example 1: Single County Focus**
```
County: Bergen County, NJ
Cities: Mahwah, Ramsey, Wyckoff
```

**Example 2: Multi-County Coverage**
```
Counties: Bergen County NJ, Rockland County NY
Cities: Mahwah, Ramsey, Wyckoff, Suffern, Spring Valley, Monsey
```

**Example 3: Specific Neighborhoods**
```
Primary: Mahwah, NJ
Neighborhoods: Fardale, Cragmere Park, Emerald Point
Adjacent: Ramsey, Upper Saddle River, Franklin Lakes
```

**Example 4: Multi-State Licensed**
```
New Jersey: Bergen County, Passaic County
Cities: Mahwah, Ramsey, Wayne, Clifton

New York: Rockland County, Orange County
Cities: Suffern, Warwick, Monroe
```

---

### Special Consideration: Character Limits

**Google Limit:** 750 characters total for service area

**If You're Licensed in Multiple States:**

> "You do want to be all-encompassing, but you still only have 750 characters. That's where ChatGPT should be able to help you out."

**ChatGPT Helper Prompt:**
```
I'm a real estate agent licensed in New Jersey and New York. I primarily serve:
- Bergen County NJ (Mahwah, Ramsey, Wyckoff, Franklin Lakes, Oakland)
- Passaic County NJ (Wayne, Hawthorne)
- Rockland County NY (Suffern, Monsey, Spring Valley)
- Orange County NY (Warwick, Monroe)

Help me format this into a concise service area description under 750 characters that's optimized for AI search engines.
```

---

### Pro Tips for Service Areas

**‚úÖ DO Include:**
- Counties (primary geographic identifier)
- Major cities/towns (high search volume)
- Specific neighborhoods where you've closed deals
- Adjacent areas where you're licensed
- Areas where you have local expertise

**‚ùå DON'T Worry About:**
- Including every tiny town
- Perfect formatting
- Exact boundaries
- ZIP codes (not necessary)

**Easy to Edit Later:**

This is one of the FEW fields you can change without triggering re-verification:
- Add new towns as you close deals there
- Expand to adjacent areas
- Remove areas you no longer serve
- Update any time

**Strategy:**

Start with your core 1 county + 3 towns, then expand monthly as you:
- Close deals in new areas
- Build expertise in new neighborhoods
- Get licensed in additional regions
- Receive referrals from new locations
