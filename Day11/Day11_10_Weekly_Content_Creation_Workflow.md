# Day 11 - Topic 10: Weekly Content Creation Workflow

## Overview

Consistent weekly content creation is the engine that keeps you visible to AI search algorithms. But creating content every week sounds overwhelming - until you have a proven workflow that makes it manageable. This guide provides a step-by-step system for creating and distributing professional content in just 30-45 minutes per week.

The secret: Create once, distribute everywhere. One piece of content becomes 7+ posts across all platforms.

## Why Weekly Content Matters

### The Recency Bias Reality

**AI Prioritization Timeline:**

| Content Age | AI Priority | Recommendation Likelihood |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **0-7 days** | HIGHEST | Maximum visibility |
| **8-14 days** | HIGH | Strong visibility |
| **15-30 days** | MEDIUM | Moderate visibility |
| **31-90 days** | LOW | Reduced visibility |
| **90+ days** | MINIMAL | Filtered out |

> "If something's a month out versus a week out, AI will choose the week every single time."

**Your Goal:** Always have content in the 0-7 day window

**Solution:** Post weekly minimum

## Step 3: Multi-Platform Distribution (15-20 minutes)

### The 7-Platform Distribution Strategy

**Open All Platforms Simultaneously:**

1. **Google Business** - Post as update
2. **Facebook** - Business page post
3. **Instagram** - Feed post or Reel
4. **LinkedIn** - Professional network post
5. **NextDoor** - Community board
6. **YouTube** - Short (if video)
7. **TikTok** - (if video and you're active)

---

### Platform-Specific Adaptations

**Google Business:**
- Paste full caption
- If video: Upload or link from YouTube
- Add call to action button
- Select category (Update/Event/Offer)

**Facebook:**
- Paste full caption
- Upload video/photo
- Tag location if relevant
- Post to stories too
- Set to public

**Instagram:**
- Shorten caption if needed (max 2,200 characters but keep under 125 words for readability)
- Upload video as Reel or photo to feed
- Add to Stories with link sticker
- Use all relevant hashtags (up to 30)

**LinkedIn:**
- Slightly more professional tone
- Can be same content or add business angle
- Use 3-5 hashtags max
- Tag relevant businesses if appropriate

**NextDoor:**
- Community-focused language
- "Local realtor sharing community insights"
- Remove some business-y CTAs
- Focus on value to neighbors

**YouTube:**
- Upload video
- Title: "[Neighborhood] Real Estate - [Topic]"
- Description: Include full caption + links
- Tags: Location keywords
- Thumbnail: Clear, readable

**TikTok (Optional):**
- Same video as Instagram Reel
- Trending audio if appropriate
- Hashtags: Mix popular + local
- On-screen text for key points

---

### Copy/Paste Efficiency System

**Browser Tab Setup:**
1. Open 7 tabs (one per platform)
2. Navigate to "Create Post" on each
3. Keep caption in notepad/doc
4. Copy/paste to each platform
5. Make minor adjustments per platform
6. Post to all simultaneously

**Time: 15-20 minutes for all 7 platforms**

## Troubleshooting Common Workflow Issues

### Issue 1: "I Don't Know What to Post"

**Solution:**
- Use the 4-week rotation (neighborhood/market/community/value)
- Pull from 52-week content library above
- Look at what competitors post (then make yours better)
- Ask ChatGPT: "Give me 5 real estate social media post ideas for [your area]"

---

### Issue 2: "I Don't Have Time"

**Solution:**
- Batch create 4 weeks of content in one session
- Use templates (fill in blanks = 5 minutes)
- Simplify: Photo + caption is fine (don't need video every week)
- Delegate: VA can distribute content across platforms

---

### Issue 3: "My Content Isn't Getting Engagement"

**Solution:**
- Add more hyper-local content (name specific streets, schools)
- Ask questions at end of posts
- Respond to every comment quickly
- Post when your audience is active (test different times)
- Use video more (gets 3-5x engagement vs text)

---

### Issue 4: "Video Makes Me Uncomfortable"

**Solution:**
**Week 1-2:** Photo posts only (build comfort)
**Week 3-4:** Record video, don't post (practice)
**Week 5:** Post first video (it gets easier!)
**Alternative:** Use screen recordings of maps/data instead of face-to-camera
