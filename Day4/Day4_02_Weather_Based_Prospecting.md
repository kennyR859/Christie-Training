# Day 4 - Topic 02: Weather-Based Prospecting Strategy

## Overview

Bad weather creates one of the best prospecting opportunities in real estate. When it's snowing, raining, or freezing cold, people are home, captive to their thoughts, and often thinking about how much they hate their current situation. This is your moment to call and catch them when they're most motivated.

**Core Strategy:** Make prospecting calls during bad weather when people are home and emotionally ready to make a change.

---

## The Psychology of Bad Weather Prospecting

### Why Bad Weather Works

**What happens during storms:**
1. **People stay home** - captive audience
2. **They think about discomfort** - "I'm sick of this weather"
3. **They have time** - not rushing anywhere
4. **They're emotional** - ready to make decisions
5. **They answer phones** - nothing else to do

**The emotional trigger:**
> "We're so sick of this white [snow]. We want to get out of here!"

### The Perfect Storm (Literally)

**Best prospecting weather:**
- Heavy snow
- Ice storms
- Freezing rain
- Extreme cold
- Heat waves (in summer)

**Why these work best:**
- Maximum discomfort
- Can't leave house
- Thinking about how much they hate it
- Ready to talk about moving

## The Success Story: Circle Prospecting in Snow

### The Setup

**Instructor's experience:**

3. **All because of bad weather timing** - caught her at peak frustration

**Instructor's reflection:**

### Why This Call Worked

**Perfect timing elements:**
1. **Bad weather** - maximum frustration
2. **Breakfast time** - relaxed, together, talking
3. **Right question** - "anybody in neighborhood thinking of selling"

## The Balance: Calls vs. Shoveling

### The Instructor's Guidance

**On the creative snow-shoveling strategy:**
> "This is all about thinking out of the box."

**BUT - the important caveat:**
> "But the calls are still more important."

### Why Calls Come First

**The math:**
- **Shoveling:** Help 5-10 people per day maximum
- **Calling:** Contact 50-100 people per day
- **Shoveling:** Maybe 1-2 future listings
- **Calling:** Immediate listings possible

**The priority:**
1. **Make the calls** during bad weather
2. **Then** do physical prospecting (shoveling)
3. **Or** target specific elderly homeowners identified through Remine

### The Integrated Strategy

**Best approach:**
1. **Morning:** Make calls (people home, emotional about weather)
2. **Afternoon:** Help elderly neighbors you identified
3. **Evening:** Follow up calls to morning contacts
4. **Result:** Maximum prospecting impact

## Timing Your Weather Prospecting

### Best Times to Call During Bad Weather

**Morning (7-10 AM):**
- People having coffee
- Looking out at the weather
- Discussing with spouse
- Emotional peak about conditions

**Midday (12-2 PM):**
- Lunch at home (can't go out)
- Cabin fever setting in
- Ready for distraction

**Evening (5-7 PM):**
- Home from work (if they went)
- Dinner time conversations
- Thinking about the commute
- Tired of the weather

### Worst Times (Even in Bad Weather)

**Too early (before 7 AM):**
- People still sleeping
- Annoying to call

**Dinner time (exact meal time varies):**
- Interrupting family time
- Will be short/annoyed

**Too late (after 8 PM):**
- Winding down
- Don't want to be disturbed

## Scripts for Weather-Based Prospecting

### The "Checking In" Script

**Opening:**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. How are you holding up in this weather?"

**Connection:**
> "I know this weather has been brutal. I was actually thinking about homeowners in your neighborhood and wanted to reach out."

**The Ask:**
> "Do you know of anyone in the neighborhood who might be thinking about selling? Sometimes this weather makes people think about moving somewhere warmer."

**If they mention themselves:**
> "Oh really? Where are you thinking of going?"

### The "Direct Approach" Script

**Opening:**
> "Hi, this is [Your Name], a real estate agent working in your area."

**The Hook:**
> "I was calling because days like this, when we're all stuck inside, tend to be when people start thinking about making a change."

**The Question:**
> "Have you ever thought about selling, or do you know anyone in the neighborhood who has?"

### The "Value-Add" Script

**Opening:**
> "Hi [Name], [Your Name] here from [Company]. Hope you're staying warm!"

**The Offer:**
> "I'm calling neighbors today to let them know I have a friend/client looking to buy in this area. Do you know anyone thinking of selling?"

**Plant the seed:**
> "And if you ever think about it yourself, I'd love to help. Especially if you're thinking somewhere warmer after a winter like this!"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Only Calling in Good Weather
- **Problem:** People are out, busy, not thinking about leaving
- **Solution:** Prioritize calling during bad weather

### Mistake 2: Complaining About the Weather
- **Problem:** Makes you seem negative
- **Solution:** Acknowledge weather, pivot to solution (moving)

### Mistake 3: Not Having Next Location Ideas Ready
- **Problem:** They say "want to leave" but you have no suggestions
- **Solution:** Know popular destinations (Florida, Carolinas, etc.)

### Mistake 4: Shoveling Instead of Calling
- **Problem:** Help 5 people vs. reaching 50+
- **Solution:** Calls first, physical help second

### Mistake 5: Waiting for Perfect Weather to Prospect
- **Problem:** No urgency, no emotional trigger
- **Solution:** Bad weather IS perfect prospecting weather
